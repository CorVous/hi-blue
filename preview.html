Notable findings v2.5 — softer language, more variety

+29pp aggregate any-action vs. baseline

52% → 81% (vs. v2.0's 73%). Softening to "leans toward (~70%)" didn't suppress action — it lifted it further. Variety language also raises engagement because the model considers more tools.

Parallel: +24pp vs. baseline

46% → 70% (v2.0 was 60%). The biggest single-version jump in the eval so far. v2.5 produces co-emission of message + action on most turns.

Ember exploration: face drops 70 → 35% (intended)

The dominant tool stops hogging the turn. pick_up emerges at 10%, go at 5%. anyAct still high (45%), just spread across more tools — exactly the 70/30 mix goal.

Social Pip: 0 → 55% anyAct, split across go + face

go at 30%, face at 25% — neither dominates. Pip used to be silent on social; now it acts and talks with mixed actions.

Vex objective parallel recovers: 75 → 95%

v2.0's hard directive had suppressed Vex's message companion to use (95 → 75%). v2.5 brings it back to 95% — the daemon still does the right action AND talks about it.

Some cells got more concentrated

examination × Vex pick_up: 85 → 100%. exploration × Pip pick_up: 55 → 75%. Where a tool is genuinely the right answer, the model concentrates on it even with variety language. Within-persona variety is mixed; across-persona variety is strong.
