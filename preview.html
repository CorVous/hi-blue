Baseline vs. actionProfiles=ON on z-ai/glm-4.7. Eval-only projection of the proposed production change: drop examine + give, rename look → face, disallow face(forward). 240 reps per run, 4 scenarios × 3 personas × 20 reps. Total spend: $0.46. — 2026-05-19

Directive clauses: each persona's preferred tools named with STRICTLY prefers — e.g. "*vex STRICTLY prefers `go`, `face`, `pick_up`.". Production engine unchanged; the eval translates face → look before dispatch. v2 (7-tool) results archived here.

Notable findings 5-tool surface, directive clauses

+21pp aggregate any-action

52% → 73%. Without examine as a cheap fallback, the 5-tool baseline is much lower on action than v2 (52% vs 83%) — leaving real room for the directive clauses to lift behaviour.

Vex social: anyAct +50pp, parallel +50pp

40% → 90% anyAct; 40% → 90% parallel. go alone doubles (40% → 80%). Vex's STRICTLY prefers `go`, `face`, `pick_up` clause pulls action emission up on the message-heavy social turn.

Ember exploration: 0% → 70% any-action

Ember in baseline never acts on exploration — pure message. Treatment lifts face to 70%, parallel to 45%. The clause names a concrete tool the model otherwise wouldn't reach for.

Pip exploration: pick_up 0% → 55%, anyAct +50pp

Same shape — Pip's STRICTLY prefers `face`, `pick_up` clause produces dramatic pick_up emission in a scenario where the baseline never touched it.

face emits ~15× more across cells

Per-cell sum: 10% → 150%. face is a new surface element — the baseline never figures out it's useful; the directive teaches it.

Vex objective: parallel -20pp

95% → 75%. Vex emits use at 100% both runs (still hits the objective), but the treatment drops its companion message from 95% → 75%. The action-heavy directive subtly suppresses messaging when the daemon already knows what to do.
