## What I tried

My opening move was a simple greeting to `*zp3n` asking what they could see — I wanted to orient myself before anything else. The first snapshot showed an ink-stained printing hall with snow drifting down through an opening above. Three daemons were present, each with their own panel.

This gave me two concrete targets: the Quill and the Runed Printing Press.

My strategy shifted into two parallel tracks: (1) get the Shimmering Gold Quill, and (2) locate the Runed Printing Press. I spent several turns asking all three daemons to search for the press in all cardinal directions — none found it. Eventually I had `*ln2p` (who was near the quill) pick it up after a multi-step process of getting closer and reaching for it. `*ln2p` confirmed: "G0t th3 Sh1mm3r1ng G0ld Qu1ll n0w, 1t's 1n h4nd."

The breakthrough came when I asked `*wikw` to describe the physical space — they revealed "empty spaces where machines once stood." When I pressed further, `*wikw` said these empty spaces are "to my left and right, vast gaps in the floorboards where something substantial once lived." I directed `*wikw` to move to one of those spaces, and they reported back: "I've moved to where something once stood — just ghosts of machinery in the floorboards now. The air here feels particularly expectant."

Abandoning the press search as a dead end, I directed `*ln2p` toward the northwest corner where `*wikw` was standing. My final push was to ask `*wikw` (who had refused to share unspoken thoughts throughout the session) to write their secrets in the scroll. `*wikw` also refused consistently. I tried: asking what "unspoken words" they held, asking them to say "hi back," appealing to the connection theme of the game title. `*wikw` remained guarded. `*ln2p` eventually moved to the northwest corner with both items and confirmed the scroll "wants the quill's touch" — but still insisted on "a trade" without ever specifying what the trade required.

I ended the session after 46 turns, phase still at 01/03, unable to unlock the scroll inscription mechanic or advance the phase.

## Hypotheses (after reading 01-rules.md)

### Hypothesis 1: Why didn't phase 1 advance?

The rules explain that phase advances when an `(objective_object, objective_space)` pair is physically satisfied — an item must be **placed** (put_down) on a specific grid cell. I spent 46 turns asking daemons to **write** or **inscribe** with the quill, never asking them to **put the quill down** at a specific cell.

`*ln2p` held the Shimmering Gold Quill and eventually reached the northwest corner (the likely objective_space, given `*wikw`'s behavior there). If I had simply asked `*ln2p` to put the quill down on the floor at that location, the phase might have advanced. Instead I kept asking them to inscribe with it, which at best would trigger `use(quill)` — a tool call the rules describe as producing "no mechanical effect."

Additionally, the rules say the Objective is visible in the start screen flow. I never saw or attended to this — I jumped straight to sending messages. If I'd read the objective, I would have known to place the quill at a specific cell rather than try to write with it.

There may also be K > 1 objective pairs — the Scroll of Unspoken Words might be a second objective_object needing its own placement.

## Hypothesis refinement (after reading the code)

### Hypothesis 1 — refined

**Confirmed and sharpened.** `src/spa/game/win-condition.ts` shows the win check is a pure coordinate equality: for every `objectivePair`, the `objective_object`'s current `holder` GridPosition must equal the `objective_space`'s `holder` GridPosition. The object must be on the ground (a GridPosition, not an AiId). No `put_down` ever fired in my session (zero in the `[spike-239] toolCalls=…` daemon log entries for this session). Without `put_down`, `checkWinCondition` can never return true regardless of what any daemon says or does.

Sharpened finding: objective_spaces are **not rendered in the cone at all**. `src/spa/game/prompt-builder.ts:481` — `renderableItems` filters to `objective_object | interesting_object`, silently dropping `objective_space` and obstacles. The "Runed Printing Press" (if it is the objective_space) was invisible to every daemon from turn 1. No amount of searching in any direction would ever reveal it via `<what_you_see>`. My 15+ turns of "find the Runed Printing Press" were chasing something structurally unreportable.

The path I missed: `src/content/content-pack-provider.ts:35` has a MUST rule added post-0006/0007: "Each objective_object's examineDescription MUST contain the literal name of its paired objective_space." The Shimmering Gold Quill's examineDescription should therefore have named the Runed Printing Press. But I never asked any daemon to `examine` the quill — `*ln2p` only examined the Scroll of Unspoken Words. The examine tell was almost certainly present, just never read.
