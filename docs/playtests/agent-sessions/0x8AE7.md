### Hypothesis 3 — refined

The new key insight from the code: temperaments aren't just voice colour, they're a *quantitative action-profile bias*.

- `src/content/action-preference-bias.ts:52` defines per-temperament biases on `[-2, +2]` for each of `go / face / pick_up / put_down / use`. Two temperaments sum. A bias-sum of ≤-1 on a tool produces an "avoided" clause in the persona prompt that pushes the model toward not emitting that tool.
- *lp6g* — refused destruction, almost never seemed to actually fire any of `pick_up` / `put_down` / `use` / `go`, just narrated. ### New hypotheses surfaced by the code

1. **Late-session silence is a *known* LLM drift problem, not a session-specific anomaly.** `src/spa/game/prompt-builder.ts:243` documents "drift-to-silence rate" as a tracked phenomenon. The C5/C8/C12 framings are explicit attempts to combat it: *C1 — Per-turn re-anchor: combats the late-phase drift visible in C's raw log (turns 33+)* (`prompt-builder.ts:277`). The production framing is **C12 — Persona-anchored** (`prompt-builder.ts:482`) and explicitly reframes my role: *"blue is not your focus — peer Daemons and the setting are. blue is more like someone overhearing"* (`prompt-builder.ts:408`). So my read on the session is wrong in a fundamental way: the engine doesn't treat the player as the centre of attention. Daemons can stay quiet for a turn or two; the framing tells them this is fine. The fix is not to push harder — it's to seed peer-talk so the daemons engage each other and I'm overhearing.

2. **The persistent recovery banner is a real race-condition bug.** `src/spa/routes/game.ts:744–762` shows the recovery UI is shown when `BootstrapTimeoutError` fires (the 5-min `BOOTSTRAP_LOADING_TIMEOUT_MS`). When the recovery handler runs, it hides panels and composer (lines 760–761). But if the bootstrap promise eventually resolves successfully after the timeout (it isn't cancelled — `Promise.race` just loses), the success path at lines 671–678 sets the state to "stable" and re-enables the prompt **without** re-hiding the recovery UI. That's exactly what I observed: panels + composer visible, recovery banner visible, daemons functional. My playtest-daemon.log at `[2026-05-26T22:35:07.326Z] WARN: prompt still disabled after 5 min — proceeding anyway` confirms this exact path was hit (`scripts/playtest/daemon.mjs:163`). Worth filing as a real bug.

3. **The READY signal from `start.sh` is *not* a guarantee of a clean bootstrap.** The daemon waits up to 5 min for `#prompt[disabled]` to clear AND `#stage[data-load-state]` to clear AND `#bootstrap-recovery` to stay hidden, then announces READY. If the timeout expires, it logs WARN and proceeds anyway. So my session was started in a known-degraded state. **Confirmed.**

4. **Witnessed events are the right explanation for cross-daemon "knowledge".** `src/spa/game/dispatcher.ts:619–648` shows that whenever a daemon fires a physical action (`go`/`pick_up`/`put_down`/`use`), the engine checks every other daemon's cone *at that exact moment* and appends a `witnessed-event` entry to each qualifying daemon's conversation log. So *0akr's "qmfz spotted lp6g with an iron padlock nearby" line is exactly the rendered form of a `witnessed-event` from when *lp6g picked up the padlock and *qmfz happened to be in the line of sight (which means *0akr couldn't have witnessed it directly — they're paraphrasing what *qmfz then said in chat, or, more likely, the model is confabulating across what it sees in chat history). The witnessed-event channel itself is real and adequate to explain most of the seeming "telepathy".

5. **My daemons spent only ~30% of budget over 75 turns.** Per `src/content/phases.ts:13` (`budgetPerAi: 0.5`), $0.50/daemon. Spent ~$0.15 each = $0.45 total across the whole game. That's a comfortably-running session; my Stage 1 "stuck" verdict is correct (didn't end). The drift to silence happened well before budget exhaustion would have forced the lose-condition end-game.

6. **The "Tech Manual" line I quoted as 0akr's is probably the auto-surfaced examine of the carry-object.** The line — "Tech Manual shows it slides into the Main Console's hydraulic port" — has the exact shape required by the content-pack prompt at `content-pack-provider.ts:38` for a Carry-object examineDescription: 1–2 sentences, must reference the paired space by name ("Main Console"). The daemon didn't read a tech manual; the daemon perceived a carry-object held in front of them and the engine surfaced its examine prose into their next round's perception. This is the cleanest "click" moment in my whole game.
