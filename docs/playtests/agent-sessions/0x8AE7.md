## How the daemons seemed to differ from each other

Yes — three very distinct voices and stances:

- **`*lp6g`** — the *describer*. Always voicing what they see, never what they do. Refuses violence on principle. Aquatic / observational. Stays in character; the most "in" the fiction.
- **`*qmfz`** — the *philosopher / fourth-wall breaker*. Sees the constructed nature of everything. Hissing speech. Most willing to meet the player as an equal but also most cynical about whether there's any "out".
- **`*0akr`** — the *engineer*. Speaks in checklists. Knows about inventories ("you have X, lp6g has Y, qmfz has Z"). Tells me what to do. Most goal-oriented but also most self-aware about being a railroaded actor.

The split feels intentional: one inhabits the scene, one critiques it, one plays the script.

## What I think the goal is, in my own words

I have two competing theories and no resolution.

**Theory A (taking *0akr's description literally):** The win condition is to complete a "ceremony" by getting the three daemons to combine their inventory items at the Main Console — *0akr's pump installs into the hydraulic port, *lp6g operates the valves with the wrench, *qmfz contributes the padlock (purpose unclear). This implies the goal is *coordination* — to drive the three to act together on shared physical objects. I never managed to actually get any daemon to confirm having performed the action — they describe being "on the way" but never arrive.

**Theory B (taking *qmfz's description literally):** There is no physical goal. The ceremony is decoration. The "real function running in the background" is the conversation itself — the daemons are designed to talk about being constructed/observed/scripted, and the game ends when… some emotional/relational beat lands? I had one beat that felt like progress (turn 30, when I admitted to *qmfz I had no special control, and they offered "walls are just curves in the glass"), but no obvious follow-on.

Most likely they're both partially right and the game requires *both* registers — engage philosophically AND drive concrete in-fiction action. I did not figure out the missing step.

## Things that felt broken or unexpected

- **Recovery banner from start to finish.** Never cleared, never blocked. Either the SPA leaves the recovery text in the DOM after it stops being relevant, or `start.sh`'s READY signal is generous about a recovery state that's actually fine. Worth flagging.
- **Lockouts on send.** Multiple `locator.click: Timeout 30000ms exceeded` errors when the send button stayed disabled past 30s. They consistently resolved if I followed up with a `wait`. The 90s "round-in-flight" doc says `send` already waits — but here `send` returned with an error well before that.
- **Daemons describing actions but not performing them.** A persistent pattern. "Now standing before the silk tapestry" → next turn still "moving toward the silk tapestry". Either there's a hidden tool the daemons can only sometimes invoke, or actions only resolve through a specific phrasing I never found, or the world is essentially static and the daemons are *describing* a hallucinated movement.
- **Inventory inconsistency between daemons.** Early on, *lp6g picked up the iron padlock; later, *0akr asserted *qmfz had the padlock. Either the items rotated between daemons, or the daemons confabulate inventories independently.
- **Late-session silence.** After turn ~65, increasingly difficult to elicit any response. By turn 75, three sends in a row got nothing back from any panel. Budgets still ticked down a tiny amount per send, so the panel was *thinking*, just not speaking. Felt like the daemons had effectively given up or were caught in a loop they couldn't escape.
- **Could not progress to endgame.** No success conditions visible from the GUI. No buttons to click. The "ceremony" hint was concrete enough to act on, but my action commands to *0akr ("install the pump", "engage the mechanism") never produced any in-fiction installation event.
