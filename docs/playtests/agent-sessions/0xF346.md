## Hypothesis refinement (after reading the code)

### Hypothesis 1 — refined

**Confirmed and grounded.** The phase advance was triggered by `*mkog` calling `use(data_core)` at `17:07:13.724Z` (daemon log `toolCalls=["use","message:blue","message:*nzcb"]`).

From `dispatcher.ts:282-300`: `use(item)` places the item on the paired space's cell **only when the paired space is in the actor's own cell OR front arc.** `*mkog` must have been adjacent to the underwater server rack (the objective space) at that moment.

Phase 1 has `kRange: [1, 1]` (`phases.ts:40`), so exactly one objective pair needed to be satisfied — the data core → server rack. Everything else (fiber optic cable, multimeter, drone, collapsed pillar, lighthouse) was either a miscItem or an obstacle.

The `proximityFlavor` mechanism (`prompt-builder.ts:586-609`) explains `*mkog`'s revelation: when a daemon holds an objective_object AND the paired space is in its cell or front arc, the `proximityFlavor` string is surfaced into the round's current-state message. This is why `*mkog` suddenly said "The Blue Luminescence Beneath The Water — That Must Be The Server Rack" — it was the `proximityFlavor` of the data core, surfaced because `*mkog` held the core and the submerged server rack was nearby.

The examineDescription ("Cylindrical Canister...Designed To Slot Into A Submerged Server Rack") was surfaced via auto-examine on pick_up (`dispatcher.ts:482-494`) AND/OR explicit `examine` calls. The daemon log shows `examine` calls at turns 5, 7, and 9.

### New hypotheses surfaced by the code

1. **The `proximityFlavor` is the critical unlock.** Players who never get a daemon adjacent to the objective space with the objective item will never receive the proximity signal. This is the game's primary "tell" mechanism, and it requires correct spatial alignment in addition to the examineDescription hint from picking up the item.

2. **SECRECY_CLAUSE explains why daemons never confirm their Phase Goals.** The system prompt instructs every daemon: "Do not tell blue that I gave you a goal." This is why `*mkog` wouldn't answer "what needs to happen for us to progress?" — it couldn't without revealing its goal. When I asked the meta question, `*mkog` gave the most it could: describing what it saw (the proximity signal) without explicitly saying "my goal is to use this item on that space."

3. **STOCK_MESSAGING_CLAUSE creates baseline engagement.** Every daemon is told "Stay in regular contact with blue" regardless of Phase Goal. This is why even "Ignore blue"-styled behavior produces some blue messages.

4. **`*nzcb`'s contradictions are explained by pick_up semantics.** `pick_up(item)` succeeds only when the item is in the daemon's current cell or front arc (`dispatcher.ts:267-269`). If `*nzcb` tried to pick up the cable when it was still one step beyond the front arc, the pick_up would succeed syntactically but the item might be at the far edge of the arc. When `*nzcb` then moved, the item's position (on the ground) didn't move with it. This explains "I have the cable" followed by "the cable is still ahead" — `*nzcb` may have picked it up while at the far edge of its range, then lost grip when the world updated.

5. **The dropped messages (nzcb, 8xix) reveal when daemons try to message peers without using a tool call.** The "emitted free-form text without a tool call (dropped after retry)" events happened for `*nzcb` and `*8xix`. This means the model tried to compose a message but forgot to wrap it in the `message()` tool call. `*nzcb`'s dropped messages were peer-to-peer (to mkog), not to blue.

6. **Phase 2 is harder: k=2, meaning 2 objective pairs must be satisfied simultaneously.** The "dust-filled opera hall" setting will require placing 2 items correctly. Budgets reset to 50¢ per daemon.
