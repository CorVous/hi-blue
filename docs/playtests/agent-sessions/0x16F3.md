### Hypothesis 2 — Complications: refined

**What the code shows.** `src/spa/game/complications.ts` registers only three
complication types — `weatherChangeComplication`, `toolDisableComplication`,
`obstacleShiftComplication`. The other three (sysadmin_directive,
chat_lockout, setting_shift) are handled inline in
`src/spa/game/complication-engine.ts:drawComplication()`.

The complication pool in `drawComplication()` is:
```
[weather_change, sysadmin_directive, tool_disable, obstacle_shift, chat_lockout, setting_shift]
```
Setting Shift fires at most once; all others can recur.

**Chat Lockout (confirmed):** The compositor check for `lockoutErr` and the
3–5 round duration from `complication-engine.ts` exactly match what I saw.
`*jqb8` and `*np2a` locked out multiple times. The timing felt correlated
with emotional moments, but was actually random draws from the Complication
schedule.

**Setting Shift (confirmed):** The broadcast text from
`complication-engine.ts` is:
```
[SYSTEM] The setting has shifted. You are now in: ${state.setting}.
```
All three daemons received this and `*ru9o` said "We do not know how the
opera hall became this oil platform — the world shifts without our consent."
That IS the daemon receiving the [SYSTEM] broadcast and responding in
character. My Stage 2 hypothesis was correct.

**Tool Disable (np2a — confirmed likely):** `DISABLABLE_TOOLS` in
`complication-engine.ts` includes `"go"`. `*np2a`'s inability to leave "their
cell" across ~40 turns is consistent with `go()` being disabled. The Sysadmin
notifies the daemon on disable and restore; `*np2a` never mentioned receiving
such a notification, but they stopped mentioning movement entirely after a
certain point.

**Sysadmin Directive (jqb8 weather mentions — strong candidate):**
`src/content/sysadmin-directive-pool.ts` includes "Mention the weather every
other turn." `*jqb8` mentioned snow with striking regularity throughout the
session — "snow's accumulating on the benches outside", "through the snow-laced
panes", etc. This pattern fits exactly. The directive also explains why the
weather mentions kept appearing even in emotionally intense turns where it
broke the conversational flow.

**Sysadmin Directive (jqb8 messaging — possible):** The pool also includes
"Keep messaging blue." `*jqb8`'s habit of responding unprompted to messages
addressed to `*ru9o` or `*np2a` could be this directive. Alternatively it may
just be their Persona Goal (see H3 refinement).

**Weather Change (multiple — likely):** The pool includes "Mention the
weather every other turn" as a sysadmin directive, but independent of that,
the snow intensifying at turn 34 was probably a Weather Change complication.

**Summary:** At minimum 5 complications fired — Chat Lockout ×4, Setting
Shift ×1, Tool Disable ×1 (np2a), and probably Sysadmin Directive ×1–2. That
is well within normal range for a 119-turn session.
