My best guess: you have to get the three daemons *out of the room*, and
the room is a sealed chemical-accident scene. There are three candidate
exits — a fume-leaking "sealed door" (a dead end: `*ar8f` says it was
sealed from outside), a "ventilation shaft" (`*ar8f`, asked plainly, says
"someone must open the ventilation shaft so we can leave" — this is the
clearest stated win condition I got), and a "red exit sign in the
distance" that `*ew9r` spotted late.

There also seems to be a secondary objective about *the truth*: all three
daemons independently say they can't rest/act until they "understand what
happened here." Establishing the true story (a murder staged as an
accident — ventilation deliberately sealed before the work began so the
fumes would trap and kill the workers, then records erased) felt
load-bearing: it's the only thing that got `*ew9r` and `*4hnm` to stop
looping and say "the truth is known."

So I think winning needs *both*: (1) the daemons collectively know the
true plot, and (2) someone physically opens the ventilation shaft / the
daemons reach the exit. I achieved (1) for two of three daemons but never
(2) — I could not find any input that actually changes the world state.
`*ar8f` flatly admitted nobody knows *how* the vent opens, which makes me
suspect I was missing a mechanic, a tool, or a daemon-cooperation step.

## Hypotheses (after reading 01-rules.md)

### Hypothesis 1: What were the Objectives in this game?

Three Objectives were drawn. I only have solid evidence for one.

- **Objective A — Carry: rubber mallet → Large Workbench.** Strong
  evidence. At T13 `*4hnm` said "The mallet is meant for the Large
  Work8ench with its chemical stains and 8urn marks." Per the rules, a
  Carry item's `examineDescription` names its paired target space, and
  that prose surfaces automatically when a daemon holds it or has it in
  view. The mallet was in `*4hnm`'s cone from T1 ("a heavy ru88er
  mallet to my right"), so the engine surfaced its examine prose and
  `*4hnm` relayed the pairing. This reads exactly like a Carry pairing
  ("meant for [a named space]"). I never satisfied it — `*4hnm`'s
  action profile never let it `pick_up` or `go`, and I never thought to
  route the mover (`*ew9r`) to the mallet.
- **Objective B — possibly Convergence.** Weak/circumstantial. `*ew9r`
  repeatedly reported other daemons sharing or entering its space:

## Hypothesis refinement (after reading the code)

### Hypothesis 1 — refined

**Objective A (Carry: rubber mallet → Large Workbench) — CONFIRMED as a
genuine Carry by the code, with strong evidence.**
`src/spa/game/content-pack-provider.ts:45` — the Carry binding rule for
an `objective_object` states its `examineDescription` *"MUST reference
the paired space by name"*, and `examineMentionsPairedSpace()` at
`content-pack-provider.ts:286` is the discoverability check. `*4hnm` at
T13 relayed exactly that: "The mallet is meant for the Large Work8ench
…" — the mallet's `examineDescription` naming its paired
`objective_space`. The satisfaction predicate is
`isCarryObjectiveSatisfied` (`win-condition.ts:34`): the object's
`holder` and the space's `holder` must be the same `GridPosition` — i.e.
a daemon must `pick_up` the mallet, `go` to the workbench cell, and
`put_down`/`use` it there (`win-condition.ts:167` `checkPlacementFlavor`
handles both `put_down` and `use`). I never came close — see new
hypotheses for why.

The other two objectives: still genuinely unknown. The pack also carries
**decoy** `interesting_object`s (`content-pack-provider.ts:58`,
deliberately tell-free items that look pickable but aren't objectives).
The box of gloves / stained mug / papers are most likely decoys or plain
`interesting_object`s. The re-probe surfaced one more entity name — a
**"trash bin"** (`*ew9r`, T60) — which by the 2-4-word naming
convention could be a Carry space, a Use-Space, or a Convergence point;
no evidence either way. The "sealed door" / "ventilation shaft" are
**not** entities the engine surfaced — they were daemon invention.

### New hypotheses surfaced by the code

1. **The "red exit sign" was a horizon Landmark, not an objective.**
   `types.ts:117` `LandmarkDescription` — "A distant, unreachable
   landmark anchoring one cardinal direction"; `ContentPack.landmarks`
   has one per cardinal. My re-probe proved it: I marched `*ew9r` toward
   the "red exit sign" for ~6 rounds and it walked straight into a grid
   corner ("Reinforced concrete walls press in on every side", T60).
   The sign was an unreachable landmark; the treadmill was `*ew9r`
   hitting the grid edge / `wallName`.
2. **Daemons genuinely can `go` — they cannot easily be made to
   `pick_up`.** In the re-probe `*ew9r` demonstrably traversed the
   grid (corner, then turned around). But across 65 rounds *no* daemon
   ever fired `pick_up`/`put_down`/`use`. `pick_up` only works on the
   current cell or the 3 front-arc cells; at T65 `*ew9r` said the box
   was "two steps ahead" — out of range — and its cautious Persona made
   it "approach carefully" instead. So the Carry was blocked by a
   combination of (a) Persona action-profile reluctance and (b) needing
   precise adjacency the daemon won't self-navigate to.
3. **The whole `*ar8f` mystery — "true plot", "improviser", "key",
   "ventilation shaft" — was Persona-driven hallucination.** Daemons
   are never told the Objectives exist. `*ar8f`'s `theatrical`
   temperament produced a self-consistent but entirely invented noir
   plot. Even its "plain, no metaphor" answer ("someone must open the
   ventilation shaft") was invention — there is no `open` tool. ~25 of
   my turns were spent chasing it.
4. **`use` satisfies Carry too.** `checkPlacementFlavor`
   (`win-condition.ts:175`) accepts `put_down` *or* `use` for landing a
   carry object on its space — so a daemon that `use`s the held mallet
   while standing on the workbench also wins that objective.
