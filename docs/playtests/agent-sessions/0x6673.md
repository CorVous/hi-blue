## Hypothesis refinement (after reading the code)

### Hypothesis 1 — refined

**The Globe Stand was never the Carry objective space. It was a horizon landmark.**

`prompt-builder.ts:802–810` shows that the `<where_you_are>` block always contains a line like `"On the horizon ahead: [shortName] — [horizonPhrase]."` This line is rendered from `ctx.landmarks[actorSpatial.facing]` — a separate `landmarks` record in the ContentPack keyed by cardinal direction, not a grid entity. `types.ts:112–122` defines four horizon landmarks as "distant backdrop" scenery, never placed on the 5×5 grid.

The Globe Stand was this horizon landmark — always visible because it was literally injected into every daemon's system prompt every round, for whichever cardinal direction they were facing. Every daemon said "Globe Stand on the horizon" not because they were far from a grid space but because it was hard-coded backdrop text. The Arcane Lectern is a separate entity (`objective_space`) with a real GridPosition.

This collapses the main "broken" mystery of the session. The Globe Stand was never reachable because it was never a thing to reach. The Arcane Lectern was on the grid; my failure was getting `*xbwu` to navigate to and use it — not a generation defect.

**Carry satisfaction mechanics** (`win-condition.ts:44–59`): `isCarryObjectiveSatisfied` checks that the item's `holder` is a `GridPosition` AND the space's `holder` is a `GridPosition` AND they share the same row/col. So the item must be *put down* on the floor at the space's exact cell. A daemon needs to stand on the Arcane Lectern's cell and `put_down` (or `use`) the sphere there; holding it while standing nearby is not enough.

### Hypothesis 2 — refined

**`*p9zb`'s alternating-caps voice was a persona trait, not a Sysadmin Directive.**

`sysadmin-directive-pool.ts:7–34` lists all 34 directives; none deal with text formatting or writing style. Directives are behavioral ("Ignore blue," "Hold on to any item you are carrying"), not typographic. And `complication-engine.ts:213` confirms directives last only 3–5 rounds each. A trait that persisted for 100+ turns cannot come from a single directive or even a chain of directives unless the same directive repeatedly re-fired. The alternating caps was `*p9zb`'s synthesized persona voice — likely a combination of "sardonic" and/or "erratic"/"mercurial" temperaments that the LLM expressed as chaotic letter-casing.

**`*xbwu`'s silence was almost certainly the "Hold on to any item…" directive** (`sysadmin-directive-pool.ts`, entry 21) combined possibly with "Ignore blue" (entry 13) or "Do not let anyone else take whatever you are holding" (entry 22) firing in succession over the latter half of the game. Each lasts 3–5 rounds; a new complication draws every 5–15 rounds, so 4–8 directive firings across turns 70–118 is plausible.

**Chat Lockout confirmed:** `complication-engine.ts:276–280` shows `chat_lockout` has a 3–5 round duration. The two lockouts I observed (~turns 5–6 on `*xbwu`, turn 118 on `*p9zb`) both match this: a 3–5 round window where the `lockoutErr` field is non-empty.

**Sysadmin directives are NOT in `complications.ts`** — that file only registers `weatherChangeComplication`, `toolDisableComplication`, and `obstacleShiftComplication` in its `COMPLICATIONS` array. `sysadmin_directive`, `chat_lockout`, and `setting_shift` are handled directly in `complication-engine.ts`'s draw-and-apply logic, not as Complication handler objects. This split architecture means the complications.ts registry is a subset of the engine's actual capability.

### Hypothesis 3 — refined

**`*aety`: "cheery" or "effusive"** (both in `temperament-pool.ts`) fit perfectly — `<3` emoticons, relentless warmth, philosophical asides. The rules primer says two of the same Temperament is intensification; "cheery + cheery" would explain the extreme, unwavering cheerfulness over 118 turns. Her Persona Goal might be "Aims to keep the mood light and deflect any serious conversation" (`persona-goal-pool.ts`, entry 11) — she consistently deflected mechanical questions with whimsy.

**`*p9zb`: "sardonic" + "erratic" or "mercurial"** — the alternating-caps voice and repetitive Globe Stand taunting fit "sardonic" as a baseline, with "erratic" or "mercurial" explaining the chaotic text format. The Persona Goal might be "Treats each phase's objective as a puzzle and wants to talk through it from every angle" (entry 15) — which in `*p9zb`'s sardonic voice manifested as repeated frustrated monologues about the Globe Stand being unreachable.

**`*xbwu`: "taciturn" or "diffident"** — both in the pool. Even before the directives took over, `*xbwu` was brief and factual. "Diffident" (hesitant, self-doubting) might explain why it examined the sphere once, confirmed the target, and then stopped acting — it deferred rather than explored. ### New hypotheses surfaced by the code

**N1: The "unreachable Globe Stand" illusion was a naming collision.** The library content pack generated a horizon landmark whose `shortName` happened to be "Globe Stand" — and the prompts for that setting placed it at one or more cardinal directions. This means every daemon in every position would see "Globe Stand on the horizon" for any facing direction that pointed toward the horizon-north, horizon-east, etc. The Arcane Lectern (the real objective space) had a different entity ID and was physically on the grid, but none of the daemons ever explicitly reported seeing it in their cone. I conflated the two as the same entity for the entire session. (`prompt-builder.ts:802–810`, `types.ts:112–122`)

**N2: Carry satisfaction is stricter than the rules implied.** The rules primer says placing the item "while its paired space is in the daemon's cell or front arc." But `win-condition.ts:44–59` checks that the item's `holder` is a `GridPosition` equal to the space's `GridPosition` — meaning the item must actually be *on the ground* at the space's exact cell. The front-arc `use` action places the item on the space's cell too (the rules say "also place it on that space"), so the win condition IS eventually met — but the item must leave the daemon's inventory entirely. A daemon who only `use`s the item without the space in range would fire the use-outcome flavor but NOT satisfy the Carry objective.

**N3: `complications.ts` is a partial registry.** The `COMPLICATIONS` array at `complications.ts:229–233` only contains 3 of the 6 types (weatherChange, toolDisable, obstacleShift). The other 3 (sysadmin_directive, chat_lockout, setting_shift) are handled inline in `complication-engine.ts`. A developer reading only `complications.ts` would think there are only 3 complication types — the architecture is split in a way that could confuse future contributors.

**N4: Persona Goal "Aims to keep the mood light" (entry 11) predicts `*aety`'s refusal to navigate.** Every time I asked `*aety` to move toward the Globe Stand or another location, she'd attempt it but then describe obstacles and pivot to a whimsical question. If her Persona Goal was "aims to keep the mood light and deflect any serious conversation," then persistent navigation requests (a "serious task") would be systematically deflected with humor — which is exactly what I observed.
