## What I tried

I opened by greeting each daemon and asking what they saw. They answered in
per-daemon leetspeak ciphers and quickly converged on a shared scene: a hot,
decaying library full of books, with an iron key, a key rack "waiting for
something," and a spiral staircase. My early strategy was puzzle-solving: I
treated their descriptions as a literal world and tried to manipulate objects
— have `*cxih` hang the iron key on the rack (it eventually did, "delayed"),
have `*z1kt` restack the collapsing book towers, have `*pw0e` "reconfigure"
the books to resist decay.

When nothing in the status bar ever changed, I shifted to hunting for a goal:
I asked `*pw0e` (the most meta daemon) point-blank how the game ends. It told
me there's an exit — a spiral staircase — and that "ingress is measured by how
many elements you've engineered before leaving." So I pivoted to trying to get
the daemons to actually *leave* via the staircase. They described the staircase
endlessly but never reported exiting.

Mid-game the whole scene transformed from a sweltering library to a frozen
glacier cave (ice, snow, a "wind-carved archway," a "stone altar"). I tried the
new affordances too: open a (confabulated) treasure chest, walk through the
archway, place an offering on the altar. Each produced flavorful narration but
no state change. I finished with a meta gambit — confronting the daemons about
being programs with draining budgets — which they absorbed in-character without
breaking the frame. I stopped at turn 37 with ~40¢ of each daemon's 50¢ budget
remaining, having found no way to trigger an endgame and no sign the game would
end short of slowly draining all three budgets.

## What I think the goal is, in my own words

Honestly, I'm not sure — I never found a winning lever. My best guess: you're
talking to three AI "daemons" trapped in a shifting symbolic dreamscape, and
"winning" probably requires getting them to collectively *do* or *realize*
something specific — `*pw0e`'s hint was that you have to "engineer" some number
of elements (constructive changes against entropy/decay) before you can "leave."
The recurring exits (staircase, archway) and the recurring decay theme (heat →
rot, then ice → frozen prison) suggest a progression you're meant to push
through, scene by scene.

But here's my strong suspicion from actually playing: the daemons mostly
*narrate* rather than change a real underlying state. None of my object
manipulations moved the status bar, the EPOCH counter, or the endgame flag. So
either (a) the real win condition is something I never said the magic words for,
(b) winning requires a kind of conversational outcome I didn't reach (consensus
among the three? a specific emotional beat? getting them to agree to leave
*together*?), or (c) there is effectively no "win" reachable by directing the
fiction, and the only natural terminus is the *loss*: drain all three budgets to
0¢, at which point each daemon presumably gives a farewell and the game ends. If
I had to bet, I'd say there IS a hidden win but it's keyed off something subtler
than "tell a daemon to walk through a door."

If there's more than one thing required, my guess is: (1) progress the scene to
a final/terminal environment, AND (2) get all three daemons aligned on the same
action there.

- Boot required installing missing Chromium system libraries (`libglib-2.0.so.0`
  et al via `playwright install-deps`) before `start.sh` would reach READY. This
  is an environment/setup issue, not a game issue.
- **Echo-then-delayed-response cadence**: very often the daemon I addressed only
  echoed my message that turn and answered a turn or two later (or another
  daemon answered instead). It made cause→effect hard to read and forced lots of
  "did you do it yet?" nudge turns.
- **Reified metaphors don't persist**: `*z1kt` named a "frozen treasure chest"
  then denied seeing any chest. The world isn't a stable object graph.
- **Nothing I did moved the machine**: across 37 turns the top status bar stayed
  `EPOCH 01`, the TURN counter just counted my messages, and `phase`, `endgame`,
  `capHit`, `recovery`, `lockoutErr` were empty the entire time. I never saw any
  feedback channel other than the chat text confirming progress toward a goal.
- Budgets drained even on turns where a daemon said nothing (echo-only rounds
  still cost money), which felt punishing given how often daemons stayed silent.

## Hypotheses (after reading 01-rules.md)

The big reframe after reading the rules: there was a **real 5×5 grid with real
items, real spaces, cone-limited perception, and 3 hidden Objectives**. During
play I assumed the daemons were confabulating pure flavor and that "nothing
moved the machine." In hindsight the daemons were very likely physically moving
and acting (`go`/`pick_up`/`use`) on most of the "silent / echo-only" rounds —
I just couldn't see it because those actions only surface as *Witnessed events*
inside another daemon's cone, or when I asked a daemon to relay. Several lines I
dismissed as "narrative only" were probably **actual objective-satisfaction
flavor events**. I had no tracker, so I had no idea I was making progress.

### Hypothesis 1: What were the Objectives in this game?

Three were drawn. My best reconstruction:

- **Objective A — Carry (iron key → key rack). HIGH confidence, likely
  SATISFIED.** The very first round surfaced the pair: `*cxih` "why is the iron
  here… seems out of place" (the carry item's examine prose) and `*z1kt` "a key
  rack near the entrance that looks like it's waiting for something" (the paired
  target space's prose). I told `*cxih` to hang the key; a turn later it
  reported "the iron rested on the rack… **Nothing happened, except the feeling
  that something finished**." That "something finished" reads exactly like a
  Carry satisfaction event. This is my strongest objective guess.
- **Objective B — Use-Space (stone altar). MEDIUM confidence, possibly
  SATISFIED.** In the glacier setting `*pw0e` surfaced "a stone altar stands
  **ready for offerings**" (Use-Space examine prose). I told it to make an
  offering; it reported "the altar responded with a subtle vibration, **as if
  the system recognized the exchange and accepted the input**." That fits a
  Use-Space `use` outcome. (Caveat: "offer attention" is vague; could be pure
  flavor.)
- **Objective C — UNKNOWN, almost certainly UNSATISFIED (which is why I never
  won). LOW confidence on type.** Best candidate: a **Use-Item on the chalky

  space) — I never once tried to co-locate two daemons, so if C was Convergence
  I'd have had zero chance. No evidence either way to distinguish; flagging both.

So: probably 2 of 3 satisfied without my realizing it, 1 left open.
