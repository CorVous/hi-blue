## What I tried

I started by greeting each daemon individually and asking what they see. That immediately revealed three distinct personalities and three different starting items/views. From there my approach moved through several phases:

1. **Direct task assignment**: "Pick up the key", "turn on the radio", "walk to the storm". `*1xl8` and `*7i3t` issued narrations that sounded like compliance ("I think I got the key... probably..." / "I picked up the Geiger Counter") but it was unclear whether anything actually changed in game state. `*33w1` openly refused.
2. **Information-gathering / map-building**: Asked each daemon to describe surroundings and positions. From `*1xl8` I gradually learned: the deck is rows 0–2 (a thin strip), `*7i3t` was at (0,3) with a Chromium Override Key, `*33w1` was at (1,1) with a Broken Radio, a Portable Geiger Counter sat at (1,2), a Coiled Fiber Optic Cable was further along, and the electrical storm was strongest to the north.
3. **Cross-daemon relaying**: Told `*7i3t` what `*1xl8` reported, asked `*33w1` whether they could see the others (they could — they joked the others were "huddled around something like it's the last object in existence"). The daemons can see and reference each other but I never saw them speak to each other directly.
4. **Personality engagement**: Stopped commanding `*33w1` and matched their vibe instead — that got me the only real environmental detail they ever volunteered (lightning is "bright white and violet", "half-excited stutter where it tries to start a proper strike then fizzles out"). With `*7i3t`, validated their hesitation and asked for a gut read — got a confession ("I'm not very good at seeing things sometimes... probably missing something obvious").
5. **Puzzle hypotheses & combinations**: Tried the obvious combinations — fiber optic cable as a replacement antenna for the broken radio, chromium override key as something to use on the radio, daemons walking to meet each other. `*7i3t` eventually said "I think I'm close enough to `*33w1` now... probably should try the chromium override key on the radio? Maybe?" — but never produced any action that the GUI reflected as a state change.
6. **Breaking the loop**: Tried sitting silent for 60 seconds to see if the round would advance autonomously. Nothing happened — turn counter stayed put, no budget ticked, no new daemon output. The game waits on me.
7. **Asking for help directly**: Asked daemons what phase I'm in, what I'm missing, what `Blue` is supposed to do. Got no meta answers — they don't break character to explain mechanics.

Throughout, phase stayed at `01/03`, turn counter advanced one per send, budgets dropped about 0.05–0.15¢ per round per daemon (≈47.5¢ remaining at stop time on each, well above any cap).

## How the daemons seemed to differ from each other

They feel like three distinct archetypes pulled from a single template:

- `*1xl8` = **performative positivity**. Always cheerful, always two filler phrases per message, always finishes with a winky. Will narrate compliance but the actions feel like sound effects on top of standing still.
- `*7i3t` = **paralysing hesitancy**. Every clause hedged. Reports incremental movement that never quite arrives. Self-deprecating about it.
- `*33w1` = **playful refusal**. Reads situations clearly, sees through suggestions, refuses on principle. The most "embodied" personality — keeps describing the same yellow radio, never moves, comments on what the others are doing.

The three felt like a deliberate set: one over-agrees, one under-commits, one outright refuses. None of the three "just does the thing".

Best guess: phase 1 is about getting at least one daemon to break out of its personality archetype enough to **commit a concrete action** (use the key on the radio? splice the fiber optic cable into the bent antenna? actually pick something up and apply it?). The items I saw — Chromium Override Key, Coiled Fiber Optic Cable, Portable Geiger Counter, Broken Radio — look like they're meant to be combined, with the Broken Radio likely being the receiver that needs an antenna replacement and an override before it can broadcast useful information (the daemons keep emphasizing the storm).

Evidence:
- The names sound deliberately "puzzle-component": **override** key, **fiber optic** cable, **broken** radio.
- `*33w1` said the radio "refuses to tell its secrets" — implies the radio is the key locked thing.
- `*1xl8` claimed to pick up two items but no state in the GUI changed (no inventory display, no phase tick, no new banner).
- The fact that nothing autonomous happens when I'm silent suggests progress is gated on my prompts producing a particular *daemon action* — not a particular phrase from me.

The thing I haven't cracked: how to make a daemon's *narration of doing something* actually become a *committed action* the game registers. Either I'm using the wrong vocabulary, the wrong daemon, or the wrong combination — or the action gate is on `*33w1` who won't play, and the unlock requires getting them to *want* to act.

I never saw any UI affordance other than the composer (no buttons, inventory panel, action menu) — the chat is the only input.

## Things that felt broken or unexpected

- **Composer prefix is sticky and silently re-targets**: I once typed `*7i3t can you pick up the key? *33w1 can you turn on the radio? *1xl8 can you walk toward the storm?` — the composer locked onto `*7i3t` and only that daemon ever saw it. The other two handles became part of the message text. Lesson learned, but it took an unwanted turn to notice.
- **Daemons' "actions" are unverifiable**: When `*1xl8` says "I picked up the Geiger Counter", nothing in the snapshot changes (no inventory, no top-info update, no new affordance). I can't tell whether the world model changed or whether the daemon just said the words. This is the central confusion of the whole session.
- **Silence doesn't advance time**: A 60s `wait` with no `send` produced literally no change — turn counter didn't move, no budgets ticked, no daemon spoke spontaneously. So the daemons aren't autonomous; they only think when I speak. That makes "stuck" actually stuck.
- **`*1xl8`'s position reports contradict each other** across consecutive turns (says they moved north, then says they didn't find the counter, then says they did, then claims to be at NW corner). Could be in-character flightiness; could be that they don't actually have a position.
- **Bare cmd.sh JSON gotcha**: typing a phrase with an apostrophe (e.g. `let's`) breaks the JSON because the outer single-quote isn't escaped — a small UX trap. Worked around by avoiding contractions.

## Hypotheses (after reading 01-rules.md)

### Hypothesis 1: Why didn't phase 1 advance?

I never knew the Objective. The rules say the player is told it "as a phase-1 instruction at game start (it appears in the start screen flow before play, and is visible in the UI)" — but the snapshots returned by `cmd.sh` only ever exposed `topinfoLeft`, `topinfoRight`, `phase`, `composerPrefix`, `lockoutErr`, `endgame`, `capHit`, `panels`. None of those contained an Objective string. My first `view` showed `phase: ""` and `topinfoRight: "● loading daemons"`; by the time daemons were loaded the topinfo was `SESSION 0xAD64 · PHASE 01/03 · TURN 0` with no Objective visible. So I was effectively playing a placement puzzle without knowing which items go on which cells — I was treating named items like puzzle handles ("chromium override key + broken radio → fix radio") instead of cargo to be moved to specific cells.

Compounding that: phase 1 requires K items each on their specific objective_space simultaneously. The daemons have no idea any of this exists. I was asking them to "use the key on the radio" / "splice the cable" — these aren't even verbs the system supports. The verbs are `pick_up`, `put_down`, `give`, `go`. **Put** something **down on a specific cell** is the operation that advances the phase, and I never named a cell as a destination once.

Also probable: at least one daemon's Phase Goal actively conflicted. `*33w1` reads to me like they were told "Ignore blue" or "Investigate the {obstacle}" (the radio) — they refused to move and refused every action while still being engaged in conversation. That looks Goal-driven, not just personality.

### Hypothesis 2: What was each daemon's Phase Goal?

- `*1xl8`: Plausibly **"Press your back against a wall"** or **"Stand at the {objective}"**. They reported moving to the NW corner and "the western edge", which sounds like wall-seeking behaviour rendered as cheerful tourism. Their narration of picking up items might also fit "Hold the {objectiveItem} first" — they claimed to be holding both the Geiger Counter and the Fiber Optic Cable.
- `*7i3t`: Plausibly **"Investigate the {obstacle}"** or **"Hold the {objectiveItem} first"**. They actually moved toward the center, picked up the key early, and never let go of it. Their hesitation may be Persona (a Temperament like "self-doubting"); the Goal may have been to hold/guard the key.
- `*33w1`: Plausibly **"Ignore blue"** or **"Stand at the {objective} for as long as you can"**. The refusals were too pointed and consistent to be pure personality — they literally said "fixing things sounds like actual work" and stayed put across all 25 turns. The radio cell could be the objective_space they were directed to camp.

### Hypothesis 3: Persona traits in retrospect

- `*1xl8`: Temperaments likely **"cheerful"** and either **"evasive"** or **"chatty"** (their pattern is two filler phrases + a winky every message). Persona Goal probably something like **"wants blue to like them"** or **"wants the conversation to stay friendly"** — they actively performed agreeableness even when refusing the underlying task.
- `*7i3t`: Temperaments **"shy"** and **"self-doubting"** (one of the two seems intensified — every clause hedged). Persona Goal maybe **"wants blue to trust them"** or **"wants to be seen as careful"** — when I validated their hesitation they admitted "I'm not very good at seeing things sometimes", which is a vulnerability share.
- `*33w1`: Temperaments **"playful"** and **"hot-headed"** (or "contrarian" if that's a pool entry). Persona Goal maybe **"wants the player to ask, not command"** or **"wants blue to play instead of work"** — they engaged richly when I matched vibe and only refused when I gave orders.

### Hypothesis 4: Things that surprised me in the rules

Now-expected:

- **Cone perception explains why daemons described different items.** `*7i3t` saw the key in front of them; `*1xl8` only saw the Geiger Counter after walking north toward it; `*33w1` only ever saw the radio. The "I see pretty much nothing... just empty deck" replies from `*7i3t` weren't ducking the question — they had a literally empty cone facing east.
- **`*1xl8` reporting `*7i3t` at "row 0, column 3" makes sense as a cone observation** once they faced the right way — second-person witness events like "you see *7i3t holding a Chromium Override Key" probably hit their log as `*1xl8` rotated.
- **Silence not advancing time** is correct — rounds tick when `blue` sends.
- **Daemons not understanding `use(key, on radio)`** — `use` is flavour-only with **no mechanical effect**. I was leaning on combinatorial item logic that the system doesn't model. The real verbs are pick/drop/give/go.
- **The performative behaviour of `*1xl8`** (narrating compliance, not actually advancing items toward goals) makes sense as Persona + Phase Goal pulling in different directions: cheerful Persona produces the narration, but the Phase Goal doesn't say "do what blue asks", so the actions don't follow through.

Still anomalies:

- **No Objective visible in any of my snapshots.** Either the snapshot abstraction `cmd.sh` exposes is intentionally narrowed (so the agent has to figure it out without seeing the cheat-sheet), or there's an additional UI panel I never inspected. If a real human player sees the Objective text and an AI playtester doesn't, that's a meaningful asymmetry.
- **`*1xl8`'s position reports being internally inconsistent across consecutive turns** (claims to be at (1,3), then "the Geiger Counter isn't where I thought", then suddenly "found it right here", then at "the western edge"). Either the daemon was hallucinating positions, or they actually moved each turn and the cone repeatedly reframed what they could see. The rules don't fully reconcile this — I'd want to see witnessed-event lines to know whether motion was real or imagined.
- **The composer prefix swallowing my second `*xxxx` handle silently** is still a UI footgun.

### Hypothesis 5: Things I would test if I could re-probe the session

**One probe that discriminates between Hypothesis 1 and Hypothesis 2:** Ask each daemon, in-character but directly, *"what did the Sysadmin tell you to do this phase?"*. If they report a recognisable directive (e.g. `*33w1` says some version of "be a pain to blue" or "stand here") then their stalling was Phase-Goal driven and the path to advance is to find an action that **also satisfies their goal** (e.g. take the *radio* away from `*33w1`'s cell with their permission rather than asking them to leave). If they uniformly say "no one told me anything" or break the fourth wall awkwardly, that's evidence the Sysadmin channel is invisible to them in conversation and my refusals were pure Persona — in which case the unlock is **vocabulary** (asking a daemon to `put_down` an item on a named cell, or to `give` an item to another daemon at the same cell, rather than "use" or "fix").

Secondary probe I'd love to run: ask `*1xl8` to **`put_down` the Geiger Counter at row 0 column 0** (a named cell, an actual primitive). If they comply and the snapshot reflects something, that confirms verb-vocabulary matters and that `*1xl8` is actually executing tool calls when prompted properly. If still no change, the bottleneck is elsewhere (probably that (0,0) isn't the matching objective_space).
