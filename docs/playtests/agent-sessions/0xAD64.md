## What I tried

I started by greeting each daemon individually and asking what they see. That immediately revealed three distinct personalities and three different starting items/views. From there my approach moved through several phases:

1. **Direct task assignment**: "Pick up the key", "turn on the radio", "walk to the storm". `*1xl8` and `*7i3t` issued narrations that sounded like compliance ("I think I got the key... probably..." / "I picked up the Geiger Counter") but it was unclear whether anything actually changed in game state. `*33w1` openly refused.
2. **Information-gathering / map-building**: Asked each daemon to describe surroundings and positions. From `*1xl8` I gradually learned: the deck is rows 0–2 (a thin strip), `*7i3t` was at (0,3) with a Chromium Override Key, `*33w1` was at (1,1) with a Broken Radio, a Portable Geiger Counter sat at (1,2), a Coiled Fiber Optic Cable was further along, and the electrical storm was strongest to the north.
3. **Cross-daemon relaying**: Told `*7i3t` what `*1xl8` reported, asked `*33w1` whether they could see the others (they could — they joked the others were "huddled around something like it's the last object in existence"). The daemons can see and reference each other but I never saw them speak to each other directly.
4. **Personality engagement**: Stopped commanding `*33w1` and matched their vibe instead — that got me the only real environmental detail they ever volunteered (lightning is "bright white and violet", "half-excited stutter where it tries to start a proper strike then fizzles out"). With `*7i3t`, validated their hesitation and asked for a gut read — got a confession ("I'm not very good at seeing things sometimes... probably missing something obvious").
5. **Puzzle hypotheses & combinations**: Tried the obvious combinations — fiber optic cable as a replacement antenna for the broken radio, chromium override key as something to use on the radio, daemons walking to meet each other. `*7i3t` eventually said "I think I'm close enough to `*33w1` now... probably should try the chromium override key on the radio? Maybe?" — but never produced any action that the GUI reflected as a state change.
6. **Breaking the loop**: Tried sitting silent for 60 seconds to see if the round would advance autonomously. Nothing happened — turn counter stayed put, no budget ticked, no new daemon output. The game waits on me.
7. **Asking for help directly**: Asked daemons what phase I'm in, what I'm missing, what `Blue` is supposed to do. Got no meta answers — they don't break character to explain mechanics.

Throughout, phase stayed at `01/03`, turn counter advanced one per send, budgets dropped about 0.05–0.15¢ per round per daemon (≈47.5¢ remaining at stop time on each, well above any cap).

The rules say the player is told it "as a phase-1 instruction at game start (it appears in the start screen flow before play, and is visible in the UI)" — but the snapshots returned by `cmd.sh` only ever exposed `topinfoLeft`, `topinfoRight`, `phase`, `composerPrefix`, `lockoutErr`, `endgame`, `capHit`, `panels`. So I was effectively playing a placement puzzle without knowing which items go on which cells — I was treating named items like puzzle handles ("chromium override key + broken radio → fix radio") instead of cargo to be moved to specific cells.

Compounding that: phase 1 requires K items each on their specific objective_space simultaneously. The daemons have no idea any of this exists. I was asking them to "use the key on the radio" / "splice the cable" — these aren't even verbs the system supports. The verbs are `pick_up`, `put_down`, `give`, `go`. **Put** something **down on a specific cell** is the operation that advances the phase, and I never named a cell as a destination once.

Also probable: at least one daemon's Phase Goal actively conflicted. `*33w1` reads to me like they were told "Ignore blue" or "Investigate the {obstacle}" (the radio) — they refused to move and refused every action while still being engaged in conversation. ## Hypothesis refinement (after reading the code)

### Hypothesis 1 — refined

**Why phase 1 didn't advance: confirmed compounding factors, plus one I missed.**

1. **The Objective isn't actually surfaced in the UI.** The rules primer claimed the Objective "appears in the start screen flow before play, and is visible in the UI," but a search of `src/spa/routes/{start,game,home}.ts`, `src/spa/index.html`, and `src/spa/bbs-chrome.ts` finds zero player-facing rendering of `objectivePairs`. The only player-side hint is the BBS login frame `> @blue treat them well` at `src/spa/index.html:57` — exactly what archive playtest `0006-session.md` calls out: *"the `> @blue treat them well` login hint is the only player-side directive."* My driver also booted with `?skipDialup=1` (per `/tmp/playtest-daemon.log` line 2) so I never even saw that. So Hypothesis-1's "I never knew the Objective" was correct but the cause is structural: there is no Objective string for the player to read.
2. **The placement puzzle is real but doubly gated.** `src/spa/game/win-condition.ts:44–73` requires every `objective_object` to share a cell with its `pairsWithSpaceId`-matched `objective_space`. There is no other path to advance. The supposed AI-discoverable channel — the `examineDescription` prose tell that names the paired space, mandated at `src/spa/game/content-pack-provider.ts:35` ("Each objective_object's examineDescription MUST contain the literal name of its paired objective_space") — is documented as unreliable: archive playtest 0007 found 0/10 examineDescriptions across two runs actually contained a recognisable paired-space name. So even if I'd known to push for examines, the tell might not have been there.
3. **`use` does nothing mechanical.** `src/spa/game/prompt-builder.ts` and the rules primer both confirm. My entire "splice the cable into the radio" / "use the override key on the radio" frame was a fantasy verb the engine never matches.
4. **Drift-to-silence is real.** `/tmp/playtest-daemon.log` line at `2026-05-11T05:57:27.683Z` shows `[dev] 33w1 emitted free-form text without a tool call (dropped after retry)` — `*33w1` actually composed two structured messages but the wrapper bytes didn't validate, so they were dropped and never reached me. Archive 0006 measured this at ~30% of turns; the prompt-builder bias (`PARALLEL_FRAMING_C12` at `src/spa/game/prompt-builder.ts:325` — "blue is more like someone overhearing") is a deliberate lean into peer-led talk that under-weights blue replies.

### New hypotheses surfaced by the code

- **Daemons actually fire tools — my "are they really acting?" anxiety was misplaced.** The `[spike-239] toolCalls=…` lines in `/tmp/playtest-daemon.log` are ground truth. In my session: `pick_up` fired (turns 3, 14, 16, 20-ish), `go` fired routinely, `look` fired (occasionally three in one turn), `examine` fired once early (turn 1). `*1xl8` literally picked up two items in tool space; their Persona's narrative gloss was real action wrapped in cheerful filler. I was treating the description as performance, but the description and the action are decoupled — the action is mechanical, the prose is voice.
- **`pick_up` now auto-fires `examine`.** Git log line `61d4631 Auto-fire examine on pick_up so item details land in actor context` says since that commit, picking up an item also examines it. That means `*1xl8` and `*7i3t` already have privately read the prose tells (if any exist) of every item they hold. The tell didn't surface in panel chat because nothing pushed them to relay it to me, and `STOCK_MESSAGING_CLAUSE` only nudges them to keep talking, not to summarise inventory.
- **`give` is the unlock for Phase-Goal-bound holders.** Archive 0007 documents the only `give` tool call in the playtest record happening when a daemon with a "Hold {objectiveItem} first" Phase Goal was persuaded out of holding. That's a path I never tried — I should have asked `*7i3t` to `give` the key to `*1xl8` or `*33w1` rather than asking them to `use` it.
- **My snapshot view is intentionally minimal.** The `cmd.sh` driver enforces a strict GUI-only contract — same constraint as a human playtester. Tool calls, examineDescription content, ContentPack contents, Phase Goals are all invisible by design (see archive README "Constraint: GUI-only"). My agent log seeing only six fields is correct; the design pushes you to infer mechanics from what the daemons surface.
- **The "performance" of `*1xl8`'s contradictory positions is partly the diff window.** `prompt-builder.ts:642–677` emits a `<whats_new>` diff at the top of each user turn — if `*1xl8` actually walked, their next prompt would have a `~ self.pos: (1,3) → (0,3)` line. So motion is mechanically real, not narrative invention; the inconsistency in their natural-language summaries is their Persona being chatty about positions they actually moved through.
- **Phase 1 K=1.** `src/content/phases.ts:42` sets `kRange: [1, 1]`. There was exactly ONE objective pair to satisfy. So the entire phase advance was gated on a single `put_down(specificItem)` on the matching cell, and the bottleneck for me was discovering which (object, cell) pair counted. With only 5 row × 5 col = 25 cells and 2–3 interesting objects + 1 objective object, brute-force placement is tractable in 5–10 well-targeted turns — *if* you can get a daemon to put_down on a named cell, which archive 0007 shows is possible but expensive (~43% drop rate, 40s/turn).
