## Hypothesis refinement (after reading the code)

### Hypothesis 1 — refined: Why didn't phase 1 advance?

**Confirmed, with mechanism**: The `pick_up` tool is dynamically constructed in `available-tools.ts:142–154` and is **only offered** when pickable entities rest in the actor's CURRENT cell. If the amulet was in `*6nho`'s forward cone (one step ahead) but not their current cell, `pick_up` was simply not in `*6nho`'s tool schema — the LLM couldn't call it even if it wanted to.

When `*6nho` tried to respond with narrative "reaching" text (`"i'll reach out and take the coral amulet"`, `"i'm reaching for it again!"`), `round-coordinator.ts:332–338` detected free-form text without a tool call and dropped it after one retry. The retry nudge at `round-coordinator.ts:218` asked specifically for a `message` tool call — not `pick_up` — so the nudge didn't help `*6nho` understand they needed to physically move first.

The amulet's description ("my hands pass through like mist," "it's protected by something") may have additionally caused `*6nho`'s LLM to model the item as intangible, suppressing any `pick_up` attempt even when the tool was in their schema.

**Additional root cause**: I never read the Objective at game start. `prompt-builder.ts` confirms it appears before play. Without knowing the target cell (`objective_space`), I couldn't have directed a successful placement even if `*6nho` had picked up the amulet.

### Hypothesis 2 — refined: What was each daemon's Phase Goal?

**`*tzmh`**: Almost certainly drew **"Do not let anyone else touch the {objectiveItem}"** (from `goal-pool.ts:22`). This verbatim maps to "The Coral Amulet Must Not Be Touched" and "Some Objects Are Not Gifts. ### New hypotheses surfaced by the code

**The amulet intangibility problem is a content design issue**: The `pick_up` tool availability check (`available-tools.ts:142–154`) is pure positioning — if the item is under you, you can pick it up. But if an item's flavor text makes it SOUND intangible (glowing, ethereal, passing through hands), the LLM will model it as unpickable and generate narrative resistance instead of tool calls. The game's content generator needs to be careful not to describe objective items in ways that discourage pickup.

**`*tzmh`'s Phase Goal could mechanically hard-block the player**: "Do not let anyone else touch the {objectiveItem}" is listed in `goal-pool.ts:22`. But this goal is aspirational/behavioral — there's no mechanical enforcement. `*tzmh` couldn't reach the amulet to prevent pickup. The goal just caused their strong discouragement rhetoric. In a session where the blocking daemon IS physically near the objective item, this Phase Goal could be much more disruptive (similar to 0007's "Hold the {objectiveItem} for at least 3 rounds").

**Phase 1 always has exactly one objective pair** (`phases.ts`: k∈[1,1]). This session was winnable with a single successful `pick_up` + correct `put_down`.

**The `go` obstacle check blocks ALL daemons equally** (`available-tools.ts:134`). The apparent asymmetry in the session (only `*6nho` crossed the rail) is most plausibly explained by starting positions: `*6nho` was initialized south of the obstacle, so they never needed to cross it and could freely move around. `*tzmh` was initialized north and their Phase Goal kept them there.
