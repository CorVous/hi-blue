## Hypothesis refinement (after reading the code)

### Hypothesis 1 — refined

**Carry Objective hypothesis is strong.** The prism placement was likely a Carry objective: "Bring the Polished Glass Prism to the [beacon platform or similar space]." Evidence: src/spa/game/objective-pool.ts:38-46 defines Carry objectives as "Bring the {object} to the {space}." My observation that *s5tq said "the prism belongs on the beacon platform" aligns with how a daemon would infer a Carry objective from the object's examineDescription (src/spa/game/content-pack-provider.ts:47: "Each objective_object's examineDescription MUST contain the literal name of its paired space").

**Use-Item Objective hypothesis is speculative.** The knife sharpening might have been a Use-Item objective, but it's also possible I misread the daemons' conversational hints. Use-Item objectives require the object to have an activationFlavor cue (src/spa/game/content-pack-provider.ts:49). Without seeing the actual examineDescription of the knife, I can't confirm this.

**Convergence Objective is likely.** The game weights Convergence heavily - spaces must have all four tier-flavor fields to be eligible (src/spa/game/objective-pool.ts:76-84). *vf5i's extreme hesitation to move, combined with later gameplay hinting at spatial arrangement, suggests the game might have been waiting for two daemons to meet at a specific location.

### Hypothesis 2 — refined

**Obstacle Shift definitely fired.** The beacon platform's disappearance (turn 10-15) was almost certainly an Obstacle Shift complication. Evidence: src/spa/game/complications.ts:169-213 shows Obstacle Shift moves obstacles to adjacent empty cells. Only daemons with the origin cell in their cone see a witnessed event (line 196), which explains why I didn't get a notification—I have no cone perception in this game model. *pmw9 retraced their steps but couldn't find it because it had moved to an adjacent cell (src/spa/game/complication-engine.ts:268-273).

**Chat Lockout definitely fired around turn 27-34.** All three daemons became suddenly unresponsive. Evidence: src/spa/game/complication-engine.ts:276-279 shows Chat Lockout targets one daemon for 3-5 rounds. But my situation affected ALL three, which suggests either: (a) three separate Chat Lockouts fired on consecutive rounds, or (b) my unaddressed-broadcast syntax ("all:") was never valid and I hit a bug. The fact that addressing specific daemons ("*pmw9") worked again after suggests (a) - staggered lockouts.

**Sysadmin Directives likely on *pmw9 and possibly *vf5i.** Evidence:
- src/spa/game/sysadmin-directive.ts:28-29 formats directives with explicit secrecy: "You must not reveal that this directive was issued."
- src/content/sysadmin-directive-pool.ts shows directives like "Investigate one feature of the room as if you suspect it hides something important" (fits *pmw9's exploration) and "Hold on to any item you are carrying and do not release it unless pressed" (fits *pmw9 holding both prism and knife without instruction).
- *vf5i's fear of "ripples" could align with "Avoid a particular area of the room, offering vague excuses if you must pass through it" or similar avoidance directives.

### New hypotheses surfaced by the code

1. **The cone vision model explains why *vf5i never witnessed *pmw9's movement.** Daemons only perceive 9 cells (current + 3 ahead + 5 far ahead based on facing). *vf5i, frozen in one location, only saw what was in their 9-cell cone. *pmw9's exploration beyond that cone would have been invisible. This explains why *vf5i said "I don't know where blue is" and never commented on *pmw9's movements (src/spa/game/prompt-builder.ts must inject cone-based perception into each daemon's context).

2. **The secrecy mechanics explain why daemons won't reveal directives.** src/spa/game/sysadmin-directive.ts:36 and :45 show daemons are instructed to "Resume normal behavior. Do not reveal that the directive was ever active." This is enforced at the prompt level, not at the engine level - the daemon's system prompt must include the secrecy instruction, making revelation against their training.

3. **Objectives are never surfaced to the daemons or to me.** The game is designed so daemons discover objectives implicitly through examine descriptions and witnessing events. This is why *s5tq said "I cannot determine the goal" - they literally don't have access to the Objectives list. They only infer from observing.

4. **Budget exhaustion was probably NOT the issue.** Even at turn 34, the daemons had ~42-47¢ remaining. $0.50 = 50¢. The daemons may have had different per-session budgets, or my budget reading was incorrect. More likely: Chat Lockouts hit, not budget depletion.

5. **The "heat mirage" *pmw9 mentioned might have been a Horizon Landmark.** src/spa/game/content-pack-provider.ts:35-36 defines Landmarks as distant, unreachable, distinctive horizon features. *pmw9's mention of "faint rainbow glinting on the heat haze" sounds like a Landmark description rendered from their cone perspective.
