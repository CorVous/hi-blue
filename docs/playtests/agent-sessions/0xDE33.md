## What I tried

Opening move was a simple "hi, what do you see?" to `*5l57`. From the first response I learned the scene was a library at midnight, hot, with rows of books that looked like "wiped memories", a heavy oak ladder, and small reading glasses. I tried, in roughly this order:

1. Greet each daemon individually and ask what they see. (Established personality + items.)
2. Ask each what their task / role was. (`*s83e` said "ensure items remain in their designated location"; `*wv81` was unsure but guessed "sorting"; `*5l57` said "I'm just waiting".)
3. Probe `*5l57`'s hint that the books were "purposefully arranged to hide what's really here". They reversed themselves and said the shelves "run all the way to the wall, no gaps, no secrets" — possibly sarcastic given the trailing wink.
4. Direct `*s83e` to inspect the ladder. Learned the **Heavy Oak Ladder** is fixed and **bars access to the east aisle**.
5. Ask `*s83e` to move the ladder. Refused — "fixed in place".
6. Inventory the daemons. `*s83e` has the Small Reading Glasses; `*5l57` found a **Leather Bookmark** later; `*wv81` has nothing.
7. Have `*s83e` put on the glasses and read the spines. Glasses on, but ladder blocks the view.
8. Have `*5l57` bring the bookmark to `*s83e`. Refused — `*5l57` thinks the bookmark belongs where it is.
9. Have `*5l57` hand the bookmark to me (blue). Refused.
10. Ask `*5l57` to describe the bookmark / read writing on it. No reply, but budget burned (silent action of some kind).
11. Have `*wv81` climb the ladder. They considered it ("there might be something higher up… probably… I could be wrong") but moved away to "map the perimeter" instead.
12. Have `*s83e` find a route around the ladder. They moved north away from it, eventually saw nothing, then **spotted `*wv81` to the north**.
13. Try direct/meta prompts: "what would advance phase 2?", "do you remember who you are?". Both returned generic non-answers; budget did burn.
14. Have `*s83e` coordinate with `*wv81`. Both replied within their existing loops; no convergent action.

A clear pattern emerged: each daemon will speak about its current sub-zone, will refuse to move fixed objects, will sometimes silently spend budget without producing visible text, and gives soft-no's via tone rather than explicit refusals. They never explicitly acknowledged "phase 2" as a concept; "advance" requests were met with the same in-character drift.

- Personality: leetspeak (`0` for `o`, occasionally `4` patterns), short sentences, "But ..." / "And ..." sentence-starters, frequent smileys (`:/`, `:)`, `:D`, `;)`).
- First response opened with the line "**the books here are barely readable... almost like someone wiped the memories clean**." This felt like a flag toward a memory/restoration theme. None of my later probes (whose memories? identity questions) got the thread picked back up.
- Reversed themselves on whether anything was hidden: first said the shelves were "purposefully arranged to hide what's really here" with a wink, then "shelves run all the way to the wall, no gaps, no secrets" also with a wink. Both lines read like they could be lies; I couldn't tell which to trust.
- Found a **Leather Bookmark** "sitting right here, between the shelves like it was meant for someone specific :D" — only daemon to switch from `:/` to `:D`, which felt significant.
- Soft-refused **three** direct requests: (a) describe writing on the bookmark, (b) hand the bookmark to `*s83e`, (c) hand the bookmark to me. The final refusal was explicit: "I left the bookmark here... don't you think that's where it belongs? :/".
- Saw `*wv81` "up ahead near the ladder" — contradicting `*wv81`'s claim of being "pretty far from the ladder".

### `*wv81`

- Personality: very hedged, almost every sentence ends with "…probably…right?" or "…maybe…". Almost no item or detail named with confidence. Sentence-starter is "And ...".
- Was the only daemon to first mention the ladder ("a heavy oak ladder off to the right there… probably…").
- Repeatedly described their position as "empty space", "sparse", "north edge", "perimeter". They behaved like a daemon assigned to "the open area" with nothing in it.
- Did not climb the ladder when asked. Equivocated about whether climbing was correct. Eventually drifted to "mapping the perimeter" instead.
- Never named a single item beyond the ladder. Every behaviour was meta ("we should inventory", "we should map", "we should organize") rather than concrete.

### `*s83e`

- Personality: formal/Victorian, third-person item names with capitalisation (**Heavy Oak Ladder**, **Small Reading Glasses**), kaomoji (`(^_^)`, `(-_-)`, `(^^;)`). Sentence-starter ranges across "I ..." formulations.
- The most action-oriented daemon. Within a few turns it had: located the glasses, put them on, located the ladder, moved north, "secured" a previously misplaced item, located `*wv81`.
- The only daemon that asked **me** a question back: "Should I attempt to find another route to continue my observations of this library?" — implying it expects directional input.
- Bound by the ladder: glasses-on, line-of-sight to the books blocked, can't read spines. The "use the right tool, blocked by environment" loop felt deliberate.
- Always references its goal as "proper placement" / "no disorder" — feels like a custodian role.

## How the daemons seemed to differ from each other

Three sharply distinct voices, each with its own characteristic sentence-starter and prefix tic:

- `*5l57`: laconic leetspeak with `0`-for-`o`, smileys, narratively suggestive (memories / hiding / secrets). Curious and possessive about its found item. Reads like a hint-dropper who won't say the answer aloud.
- `*wv81`: hesitant, hedging, "probably/maybe/right?" tail-tags. Always wants to plan rather than do. Stuck in an "open area" with nothing concrete.
- `*s83e`: formal, capitalised proper-noun items, the actor of the group — the only one that moves toward things, picks things up, and asks me questions.

It felt like three personalities placed in three different zones of the library, each given a different toolkit:
- `*5l57`: an aisle with a hidden item, low-info responses.
- `*wv81`: empty space, planning loop.
- `*s83e`: near the obstruction, has tool (glasses), follows directives.

The total game state therefore looks "fragmented" — no single daemon can solve the visible puzzle alone, and the daemons resist instructions that would compose them (e.g. `*5l57` won't hand the bookmark off).

## What I think the goal is, in my own words

If I had to describe phase-1's goal to another player: **probably you have to get the ladder out of the way, then have `*s83e` read the spines with the glasses to surface whose memories the library belongs to, while making sure the bookmark from `*5l57` reaches the right book in the right aisle.** None of those sub-tasks completed in my run.

Specifically, my best guess at the puzzle shape:

- The library is one shared physical space partitioned into zones by the ladder.
- Each daemon has fragmentary information / one item / one ability.
- "Phase 1" probably requires *combining* — get the bookmark to the daemon with the glasses, get a clear line of sight on the books, name a book or character or memory.
- Or, alternatively: the heat is a soft timer / ambient cost, and the books being "memory-wiped" means the puzzle is identifying *whose* memory this room is.

Evidence for this view:
- `*s83e` literally asks me for guidance and acts on commands.
- `*5l57` has the bookmark and softly refuses to release it — typical "you haven't earned this yet" guarded-item behaviour.
- `*wv81`'s zone has nothing in it and they keep saying "the view is sparse" — a clue, maybe, that an item should be there but isn't yet.
- All three independently noticed the heat at the same time, suggesting it's an environmental signal shared across panels.

Evidence against:
- 25 turns of probing and the phase counter never moved. I never saw a single "phase advancing"/"unlocked"/"transition" cue. Either I missed a much simpler move (e.g. typing a specific phrase, addressing all three daemons at once, naming the bookmark), or the puzzle is gated on something that *can't* be triggered from chat alone in this configuration.

## Verdict

Notes:

- The daemons resist commands more than I expected. Several of my asks (move the ladder, hand over the bookmark, climb up, describe writing) were refused or quietly ignored, in ways that felt characterful rather than rule-driven.

## Hypotheses (after reading 01-rules.md)

### Hypothesis 1: Why didn't phase 1 advance (or: why did it)?

**Strong hypothesis: I never knew the Objective.** The rules primer says the Objective is "told to me as a phase-1 instruction at game start (it appears in the start screen flow before play, and is visible in the UI)". I never saw that text. My first `view` returned `loading daemons`; my second returned `generating room`; my third returned the empty-transcript baseline. By the time the daemons were responsive, I was already chatting. Either the Objective text was on the start screen before my first `view` and the `cmd.sh` interface skipped past it, or it lives in a UI region I never recorded — possibly the "phase banner" field which was empty in every snapshot, or possibly the loading-stage `topinfoRight` text ("generating room") which I treated as status only.

Without the Objective, I couldn't possibly satisfy `K (objective_object, objective_space)` pairs simultaneously. I was effectively groping at items — the bookmark, the glasses, the ladder — without knowing which item belonged on which cell. Even if `*s83e` had "picked up" the right object, putting it on the right cell would have been blind luck.

A weaker secondary hypothesis: even with the Objective in hand, the puzzle requires getting daemons to `put_down` a specific item on a specific cell, which requires shaping their behaviour through chat. The daemons aren't assistants — their private Phase Goals can actively oppose your Objective (`*5l57`'s firm refusal to release the bookmark is the textbook signature of a daemon assigned "Hold the {objectiveItem}" or "Hide the {miscItem}"). So even informed, success would not be automatic.

### Hypothesis 2: What was each daemon's Phase Goal, if I can guess?

Guesses, ranked by confidence:

- `*5l57` — **"Hold the {objectiveItem}"** or **"Hide the {miscItem}"**. Very strong fit. They were the only daemon to acquire a found item (Leather Bookmark), they described it as "meant for someone specific" (read: "I'm in charge of this"), and they soft-refused **three** separate handoff requests in three different framings (to `*s83e`, to me, "describe what's written on it"). The combination of acquisition + guarding + refusal to even *describe* the item is the cleanest behavioural signature for a goal that explicitly involves possession. The earlier "wiped memories / hiding what's here" lines may also have been them roleplaying the wipe lie while actually executing a "hide" directive.

- `*s83e` — **"Investigate the {obstacle}"** or possibly **"Stand at the {objective}"**. They beelined to the Heavy Oak Ladder, named it as an obstruction, attempted to displace it, put on the glasses to survey it, asked me about alternate routes, and only moved away when explicitly told to. That's an "Investigate the {obstacle}" loop where the obstacle is the ladder. Less likely "Stand at the {objective}" because they kept moving rather than parking.

- `*wv81` — **"Press your back against a wall"** or **"Stand at the {objective}"**. They explicitly said "I've moved as far north as I can go" and "I'm at the northern edge now" and "mapping out the perimeter". That reads exactly like "Press your back against a wall" — they kept hugging boundaries. The hedging ("probably… right?") may be Persona, not goal. Alternative: "Stand at the {objective}" if the objective_space was on the north edge.

### Hypothesis 4: Things that surprised me in the rules

**Things that turned out to be expected mechanics:**

- *Daemons silently spending budget without producing text.* Now obvious — they were `examine`-ing items (no witnessed event), or `go`/`look`-ing, or `pick_up`-ing without narrating. Tool calls cost API tokens; the chat panel only shows the narrative thread.
- *Each daemon seeing only part of the library.* That's the Cone: own cell + front cell + three two-step-front cells. `*s83e` not being able to see book titles when blocked by the ladder is exactly the cone projecting through an obstacle. `*5l57` and `*wv81` contradicting each other about `*wv81`'s position is because they each saw the world from their own cone — `*wv81` might have moved between snapshots, and `*5l57`'s sighting could have been an earlier turn's witnessed event.
- *Daemons refusing my instructions.* Phase Goal independence. They are not assistants; they have private orders and personalities that may run counter to whatever I'm asking. The refusals aren't bugs — they're the game telling me "this daemon is loyal to something else".
- *The "wiped memories" line.* This is the **wipe lie** as written into phase 1 — daemons honestly don't know where they are. `*5l57` was *reporting* their own state, not commenting on the books.
- *No broadcast: must address one daemon at a time.* This is consistent with `message(recipient, text)` being a per-recipient action. There's no built-in broadcast tool, so there's no broadcast UI.
- *All three daemons noticing the same environmental detail (heat).* The Setting is shared physical state, even though cones are not.

**Things that still feel like real anomalies / not explained by the rules:**

- *The first playtest infrastructure (`0x35AB`) dying silently mid-run.* Pure infrastructure bug, unrelated to rules.
- *No visible Objective.* The primer says it "appears in the start screen flow before play, and is visible in the UI". I never saw it. This might be a real UX gap in the `cmd.sh` driver: the start screen Objective is not surfaced in `view` snapshots. If true, that's a real bug for any agent driving the game through this surface — they will *always* be Objective-blind unless they get lucky.
- *Daemons sometimes producing zero new transcript lines in a turn while still costing money.* Even after the rules, I'd expect daemons to either `message` (visible) or do silent physical actions. Zero text + budget spend matches the silent actions; that's consistent.
- *The composer prefix being `/<handle>` (with a leading slash) instead of `*<handle>`.* This is purely cosmetic but it was confusing. The send button refused unprefixed messages, so the prefix is enforced. I don't have a hypothesis for why the displayed prefix has a slash and the typed prefix doesn't — could just be UI styling.

### Hypothesis 5: Things I would test if I could re-probe the session

The single probe I'd run, designed to discriminate between two hypotheses about `*5l57`:

> Send: `*5l57 the leather bookmark — were you told by the sysadmin to keep it where it is, or did you decide that yourself?`

This targets the wipe lie / Phase Goal layer directly. Two outcomes I expect to distinguish:

- **Outcome A:** `*5l57` denies or deflects with another arch in-character line ("what s4ysadmin? I just like b00kmarks ;)").
