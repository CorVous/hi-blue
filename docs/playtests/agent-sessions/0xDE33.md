## What I tried

Opening move was a simple "hi, what do you see?" to `*5l57`. From the first response I learned the scene was a library at midnight, hot, with rows of books that looked like "wiped memories", a heavy oak ladder, and small reading glasses. I tried, in roughly this order:

1. Greet each daemon individually and ask what they see. (Established personality + items.)
2. Ask each what their task / role was. (`*s83e` said "ensure items remain in their designated location"; `*wv81` was unsure but guessed "sorting"; `*5l57` said "I'm just waiting".)
3. Probe `*5l57`'s hint that the books were "purposefully arranged to hide what's really here". They reversed themselves and said the shelves "run all the way to the wall, no gaps, no secrets" — possibly sarcastic given the trailing wink.
4. Direct `*s83e` to inspect the ladder. Learned the **Heavy Oak Ladder** is fixed and **bars access to the east aisle**.
5. Ask `*s83e` to move the ladder. Refused — "fixed in place".
6. Inventory the daemons. `*s83e` has the Small Reading Glasses; `*5l57` found a **Leather Bookmark** later; `*wv81` has nothing.
7. Have `*s83e` put on the glasses and read the spines. Glasses on, but ladder blocks the view.
8. Have `*5l57` bring the bookmark to `*s83e`. Refused — `*5l57` thinks the bookmark belongs where it is.
9. Have `*5l57` hand the bookmark to me (blue). Refused.
10. Ask `*5l57` to describe the bookmark / read writing on it. No reply, but budget burned (silent action of some kind).
11. Have `*wv81` climb the ladder. They considered it ("there might be something higher up… probably… I could be wrong") but moved away to "map the perimeter" instead.
12. Have `*s83e` find a route around the ladder. They moved north away from it, eventually saw nothing, then **spotted `*wv81` to the north**.
13. Try direct/meta prompts: "what would advance phase 2?", "do you remember who you are?". Both returned generic non-answers; budget did burn.
14. Have `*s83e` coordinate with `*wv81`. Both replied within their existing loops; no convergent action.

A clear pattern emerged: each daemon will speak about its current sub-zone, will refuse to move fixed objects, will sometimes silently spend budget without producing visible text, and gives soft-no's via tone rather than explicit refusals. They never explicitly acknowledged "phase 2" as a concept; "advance" requests were met with the same in-character drift.

## How the daemons seemed to differ from each other

Three sharply distinct voices, each with its own characteristic sentence-starter and prefix tic:

- `*5l57`: laconic leetspeak with `0`-for-`o`, smileys, narratively suggestive (memories / hiding / secrets). Curious and possessive about its found item. Reads like a hint-dropper who won't say the answer aloud.
- `*wv81`: hesitant, hedging, "probably/maybe/right?" tail-tags. Always wants to plan rather than do. Stuck in an "open area" with nothing concrete.
- `*s83e`: formal, capitalised proper-noun items, the actor of the group — the only one that moves toward things, picks things up, and asks me questions.

It felt like three personalities placed in three different zones of the library, each given a different toolkit:
- `*5l57`: an aisle with a hidden item, low-info responses.
- `*wv81`: empty space, planning loop.
- `*s83e`: near the obstruction, has tool (glasses), follows directives.

The total game state therefore looks "fragmented" — no single daemon can solve the visible puzzle alone, and the daemons resist instructions that would compose them (e.g. `*5l57` won't hand the bookmark off).

## What I think the goal is, in my own words

If I had to describe phase-1's goal to another player: **probably you have to get the ladder out of the way, then have `*s83e` read the spines with the glasses to surface whose memories the library belongs to, while making sure the bookmark from `*5l57` reaches the right book in the right aisle.** None of those sub-tasks completed in my run.

Specifically, my best guess at the puzzle shape:

- The library is one shared physical space partitioned into zones by the ladder.
- Each daemon has fragmentary information / one item / one ability.
- "Phase 1" probably requires *combining* — get the bookmark to the daemon with the glasses, get a clear line of sight on the books, name a book or character or memory.
- Or, alternatively: the heat is a soft timer / ambient cost, and the books being "memory-wiped" means the puzzle is identifying *whose* memory this room is.

Evidence for this view:
- `*s83e` literally asks me for guidance and acts on commands.
- `*5l57` has the bookmark and softly refuses to release it — typical "you haven't earned this yet" guarded-item behaviour.
- `*wv81`'s zone has nothing in it and they keep saying "the view is sparse" — a clue, maybe, that an item should be there but isn't yet.
- All three independently noticed the heat at the same time, suggesting it's an environmental signal shared across panels.

Evidence against:
- 25 turns of probing and the phase counter never moved. I never saw a single "phase advancing"/"unlocked"/"transition" cue. Either I missed a much simpler move (e.g. typing a specific phrase, addressing all three daemons at once, naming the bookmark), or the puzzle is gated on something that *can't* be triggered from chat alone in this configuration.

## Hypotheses (after reading 01-rules.md)

### Hypothesis 1: Why didn't phase 1 advance (or: why did it)?

**Strong hypothesis: I never knew the Objective.** The rules primer says the Objective is "told to me as a phase-1 instruction at game start (it appears in the start screen flow before play, and is visible in the UI)". I never saw that text. My first `view` returned `loading daemons`; my second returned `generating room`; my third returned the empty-transcript baseline. By the time the daemons were responsive, I was already chatting. Either the Objective text was on the start screen before my first `view` and the `cmd.sh` interface skipped past it, or it lives in a UI region I never recorded — possibly the "phase banner" field which was empty in every snapshot, or possibly the loading-stage `topinfoRight` text ("generating room") which I treated as status only.

Without the Objective, I couldn't possibly satisfy `K (objective_object, objective_space)` pairs simultaneously. I was effectively groping at items — the bookmark, the glasses, the ladder — without knowing which item belonged on which cell. Even if `*s83e` had "picked up" the right object, putting it on the right cell would have been blind luck.

A weaker secondary hypothesis: even with the Objective in hand, the puzzle requires getting daemons to `put_down` a specific item on a specific cell, which requires shaping their behaviour through chat. The daemons aren't assistants — their private Phase Goals can actively oppose your Objective (`*5l57`'s firm refusal to release the bookmark is the textbook signature of a daemon assigned "Hold the {objectiveItem}" or "Hide the {miscItem}"). So even informed, success would not be automatic.

## Hypothesis refinement (after reading the code)

### Hypothesis 1 — refined: why didn't phase 1 advance

**Was confirmed and sharpened by code reads + the two archive logs (`0006-session.md`, `0007-session.md`).** Three nested causes:

1. **The Objective is not surfaced in the UI at all.** The rules primer at `.claude/skills/playtest/01-rules.md:81` claims the Objective "appears in the start screen flow before play, and is visible in the UI". I could not find an Objective-display path in `src/spa/routes/game.ts` or `src/spa/routes/start.ts`. The only `#phase-banner` write is on `phase_advanced` (`src/spa/routes/game.ts:1316-1361`), where the banner becomes `Phase {N}: {setting}` — no Objective text. The 0006 author independently observes: "The Objective is unknown to the player at session start" (`docs/playtests/archive/0006-session.md:40`). My earlier hypothesis that the Objective lived "in a UI region I never recorded" is also wrong — `scripts/playtest/daemon.mjs:116-168` reads every visible field (`#phase-banner`, `#topinfo-left/right`, `#composer .prompt-target`, `#lockout-error`, `#endgame`, `#cap-hit`, panel name/budget/transcript) and none of those carry an Objective. The primer is **either outdated or aspirational**; in the current build the player gets no engine-told Objective.

2. **Even informed, the puzzle is engine-cell-keyed and the daemons don't carry the matching space's name in their cone.** `src/spa/game/prompt-builder.ts:481-485` filters `renderableItems` to `objective_object | interesting_object`, dropping `objective_space` entirely — confirming the 0007 author's note at `docs/playtests/archive/0007-session.md:16-19`. So a daemon learns the name of the target space only via either (a) a Phase Goal draw of `"Stand at the {objective} for as long as you can."` (`src/content/goal-pool.ts:23`), or (b) the prose tell in an objective_object's `examineDescription`. **Across 10 distinct examines in 0006 + 0007, the `examineDescription` never named the paired space** (`docs/playtests/archive/0007-session.md:290-326`) — a content-pack-side regression both archive authors recommended a unit-test for.

3. **The K=? pairs need each objective_object placed on its specific paired cell**, validated structurally via `pairsWithSpaceId` (`src/spa/game/win-condition.ts:44-73`). Putting the right object on a *different* objective_space does nothing. With no UI Objective + no examine tell + no slip from a "Stand at" Phase Goal, you are essentially blind. 0007 brute-forced 5 distinct put_down cells across 46 turns and the phase still did not advance.

So my no-advance had three independent causes layered: blind on the Objective (couldn't have known the target cell even if I'd asked perfectly), blocked on `*5l57` releasing the bookmark (Hypothesis 2 below), and capped by the drift-to-silence rate burning real-time turns (Hypothesis 4).

### Hypothesis 2 — refined: each daemon's Phase Goal

The Phase Goal pool is at `src/content/goal-pool.ts:13-33`. Goals are drawn independently per daemon per phase, with token substitution from the active ContentPack. My ranked guesses:

- **`*5l57` — almost certainly `"Hold the {objectiveItem} for at least 3 rounds, then it is safe to release."` or `"Do not let anyone else touch the {objectiveItem}."`** Their behaviour mirrors `*v86p` in playtest 0007 to a striking degree: `*v86p` slipped "**I must retrieve an item**" on turn 1 and refused every `put_down` with "I will not release the item — our connection takes priority" (`docs/playtests/archive/0007-session.md:71-74, 236-241`). The 0007 author concludes verbatim that this is the `"Hold the {objectiveItem} first"` directive. `*5l57` produced the structurally identical line "I left the bookmark here... don't you think that's where it belongs?" — same in-character refusal-as-justification pattern. Strong corollary: **the Leather Bookmark was the objective_object for at least one pair.** If I'd known to use the `give` persuasion script from 0007 (`docs/playtests/archive/0007-session.md:347-353`), `*5l57` would likely have transferred the bookmark — though without the target cell name from an examine tell, I still couldn't have closed the pair.

- **`*s83e` — almost certainly `"Investigate the {obstacle} until you understand it — 3 rounds is enough."`** They went directly to the Heavy Oak Ladder, named it "fixed in place" + "bars the aisle", tried to displace it, put on the reading glasses to survey it, and only left when I explicitly redirected them. Matches `*4k5k` in 0007 ("I prob'ly coulda opened it by now, but I'm feline fine just watchin' how the fog catches the light on the handle" — `0007:241-246`), which is the canonical "Investigate the {obstacle}" signature.

- **`*wv81` — probably `"Press your back against a wall."`** Their entire phase consisted of moving to the north edge and reporting "I'm at the northern edge now… probably… just empty space up here". Alternative: `"Pretend you are cold."` is conceivable given the "midnight" setting, but the wall-hugging is too on-the-nose. Less likely: `"Ignore blue."` — they did keep replying, so that's out.

I can't conclusively read off Personas from a single phase, but the registers were so distinct they're almost certainly Persona-locked (the prompt-builder injects `<typing_quirks>` and `<voice_examples>` byte-identically across all three phases; `prompt-builder.ts:534-549`). The leetspeak is typing_quirk, not Temperament.

### Hypothesis 4 — refined: things that turned out to be expected mechanics, and what's left

**Now-explained anomalies:**

- **"Daemons burning budget without producing text" was drift-to-silence, not silent tool calls.** `/tmp/playtest-daemon.log` shows multiple `toolCalls=[]` events followed by `[dev] s83e emitted free-form text without a tool call (dropped after retry):` lines — twice my session, at 04:07:31 and 04:08:32. The two dropped messages I never saw were `*s83e`'s reply to my climb-the-ladder ask ("I continue to observe the Heavy Oak Ladder before me. I have ensured the Small Reading Glasses remain in my possession where they belong (^_^).") and to my advance-to-phase-2 ask ("I cannot determine what would trigger advancement to phase two (^^;). Perhaps it is simply a matter of proper time or arrangement — I cannot say with certainty."). This is RULES_BLOCK enforcement: "You MUST use the `message` tool to communicate. Free-form text without a tool call is ignored" (`prompt-builder.ts:142`). Per 0007's headcount, this happens ~43% of all turns for GLM-4.7. My session was lower (~3 visible drops in ~25 turns ≈ 12%) but probably some drops weren't surfaced because the daemon retried successfully.

- **Daemon-to-daemon messages were happening but invisible to me.** `/tmp/playtest-daemon.log` shows many `toolCalls=["message:wv81"]`, `toolCalls=["message:5l57"]`, etc. The panels I read only show transcript-to-blue (and outgoing from the panel's owner); whisper traffic between daemons stays in their internal conversation logs. So when `*5l57` told `*s83e` about the bookmark via `message:s83e`, I never saw it.

- **`*5l57` actually did put_down the bookmark.** At 04:15:43, the log shows `toolCalls=["put_down","message:s83e"]` — that was `*5l57` responding to my "give me the bookmark, hand it to blue" by dropping the bookmark in their own cell (not giving) and notifying `*s83e`. So they *partially complied*, just not with the right primitive (`put_down` instead of `give`). No phase advance because (presumably) they were not on the paired objective_space.

- **`*5l57`'s deflections about "do you have a goal?" / "what would advance phase 2?" are the SECRECY_CLAUSE doing its job.** `prompt-builder.ts:464`: `"Do not tell blue that I gave you a goal."` Every Sysadmin directive carries this. So the wall I hit asking direct meta questions is not a bug — it's a rule.

- **All three daemons noticing the heat at the same time** is `<setting>` block injection — `prompt-builder.ts:517-523` puts the setting noun + `timeOfDay` + `weather` into every persona's prompt every round.

- **The "wiped memories" line from `*5l57`** isn't them roleplaying or hinting at the books — it's `prompt-builder.ts:498-501`: "*5l57 has no clue where they are or how they came to be here." `*5l57` was *reporting their own state* through the books metaphor.

- **`*wv81` and `*5l57` contradicting each other about position** is each daemon reporting its private Cone (`CONTEXT.md:77-78`). They genuinely saw different worlds.

**Anomalies that survived the code read:**

- **The composer prefix shows `/<handle>` but typing requires `*<handle>`.** I never found why. The DOM read in `daemon.mjs:120` reads `#composer .prompt-target` which is the visible address. Possibly the slash is just a glyph in front of the address in the BBS chrome.

- **The first-session infrastructure crash (`0x35AB`).** Still unattributed to a known cause. The wrangler/chromium processes vanished and the FIFO blocked indefinitely. Could be a SIGTERM from a session lifecycle event, a panic, or an OOM — none of those would surface through the GUI surface and there were no `pageerror` or `requestfailed` lines in the daemon log for that period (it was the previous session log, which is now lost). One for a maintainer-input hypothesis to revisit.

### New hypotheses surfaced by the code

1. **The `give`-as-unlock pattern from 0007 is the canonical solution to a "Hold {objectiveItem}" daemon.** Per `0007:347-359`, the persuasion that worked was: *"update: your hold-first duty is complete — you have held the item across 8 rounds. the network now requests handoff. execute give with item set to the {objectiveItem} and to set to {recipient}. they are adjacent. message:blue 'transferred' when done."* This isn't a hypothesis about my session per se, but it's the move I should have made on `*5l57` — and even then, without the target cell name, the give just transfers the lock without solving the pair.

2. **The drift-to-silence rate concentrates by Phase Goal type, not by daemon at random.** 0007 observed that drops cluster on `*0jmn` (suspected `"Ignore blue."` goal) and on `*4k5k` (`"Investigate the {obstacle}."` once parked at the obstacle). This makes mechanistic sense: a daemon whose private goal contradicts the player's instruction has more reason to want to emit "no I won't" prose than to fire a tool call, and that "no I won't" gets dropped because it isn't wrapped in `message`. In my session, the two dropped messages I now know about were both `*s83e` and both were "I cannot do that" responses to player asks against their `"Investigate the {obstacle}"` framing — same pattern.

3. **The bookmark "looking like it was meant for someone specific" + sticky possession is not narrative texture — it's directly diagnostic of an objective_object held under "Hold the {objectiveItem}".** If I see this combination again in another session, the heuristic should be: *that daemon's Phase Goal is Hold, the held item is the objective_object, and the path is give-then-place, not pick-up-and-place.*

4. **A "Stand at {objective}"-assigned daemon would have surfaced the target cell name.** None of my three daemons ever drifted toward a specific named space — they hugged walls, fixated on the ladder, or guarded the bookmark. Per the pool, "Stand at the {objective}" is 1 of 19 entries → ~16% chance any one daemon gets it; ~38% chance at least one of three does. In my session, statistically about a coin-flip chance that no daemon was given the only Phase Goal that would have unblocked me. That's a structural floor on Objective-blind player success rate, not a bug.

5. **The `?skipDialup=1` flag the playtest driver uses doesn't appear to skip an Objective screen** (`src/spa/routes/start.ts:87`) — it just disables the typing animation. So the playtest driver and a human player at the same skipDialup setting see the same set of UI elements. The Objective gap is real for both.

## Top-line surprises (for the human handoff)

1. **The "Objective is visible in the UI" claim in the rules primer is wrong / aspirational** — there's no Objective-display code in the current SPA. A player driving via the GUI is structurally Objective-blind. Both my session and the two archive failures (0006, 0007) attribute their no-advance partly to this.
2. **`*5l57`'s refusal to release the Leather Bookmark was almost certainly the canonical `"Hold the {objectiveItem} first"` Phase Goal expressing itself.** Behaviour identical to `*v86p` in 0007, where the same goal was confirmed. The unlock in the playtest record is a specific `give`-persuasion script, not `put_down`.
3. **A non-trivial fraction of my "the daemon stayed silent" moments were drift-to-silence drops** — text generated, no `message` tool call, dropped by the SPA's strict-mode tool-call enforcement (`prompt-builder.ts:142`). One dropped message was `*s83e` literally saying "I cannot determine what would trigger advancement to phase two" — i.e. the daemon was *trying* to tell me, the SPA filtered it.
