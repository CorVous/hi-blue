## How the daemons seemed to differ from each other

They felt like three genuinely distinct personalities, and each one is built
around a single, specific cognitive *flaw* expressed through a consistent
speech style:

- `*y464` — **the pedant.** Cannot commit to a claim; hedges everything;
  blocked specifically on *irreversible* action. Speech: trailing ellipses,
  "technically imprecise", kaomoji.
- `*r7pn` — **the unfinisher.** Cannot complete anything; endlessly "trying";
  but scrupulously honest (won't claim a success it didn't get). Speech: the
  ")(" tic, warm, rambling, conflict-averse.
- `*28iw` — **the over-observer.** Cannot act before observation is
  "complete", which it never is. Speech: parenthetical caveat + "what I mean
  is" restatement.

The flaws are complementary: one won't assert, one won't finish, one won't
act. That symmetry feels deliberate, not accidental.

## What I think the goal is, in my own words

You need to satisfy three hidden objectives ("obj N/3 satisfied" in the
header). I only ever satisfied one, so I'm partly guessing.

My best read: each objective is satisfied when a daemon performs a
**decisive, completed interaction with an operable object** — and the
challenge is that each daemon's personality flaw blocks exactly that. The
single objective I got (0/3 → 1/3) coincided with `*y464` flipping the
emergency switch box: a simple binary mechanism, and the one moment `*y464`
overcame its "won't commit" flaw because I told it to stop analysing and
just flip it.

So I'd tell another player: don't fight the room, fight the daemons'
personalities. Find the action each daemon's flaw is blocking, and find the
words that get that *specific* daemon past its flaw. For the pedant, frame
the action as simple/reversible and forbid analysis. For the unfinisher and
the over-observer I never found the key — `*r7pn` won't claim success it
hasn't earned and the world never let it earn one; `*28iw` won't move at
all. I suspect there are three operable objects (one switch-like thing per
objective) and the win is operating all three, but I could only find/operate
one.

The competing possibility: the game booted broken (see below) and the
objective layer simply doesn't resolve, so 1/3 was as far as anyone could
get. I genuinely can't distinguish these two from inside Stage 1.

## Hypothesis refinement (after reading the code)

### Hypothesis 1 — refined

Re-probing the live session in Stage 3 unlocked the **dev inspector world
map** (`src/spa/dev-inspector/world-map.ts`) — the small ASCII grid in the
screenshot's top-right corner. Its glyph legend
(`world-map.ts:150-160`): `^v><` daemon+facing, `##` obstacle, `**` carry
object on its space, `*` carry object alone, `+` objective space alone,
`o` interesting object, `.` floor.

Cropping that grid at high resolution showed the actual objective layout
(glacier pack B): **one lone `*`** (a carry object, *not* on a space → its
Carry objective is unsatisfied), **three adjacent `+` objective spaces**,
one `o`, and three `##` obstacles.

Grounding that against `objective-record-builder.ts`: a Carry objective
contributes one object + one space, Use-Space and Convergence contribute one
space each, Use-Item contributes only an item (rendered `o`). Three `+`
spaces + one `*` object ⇒ the three Objectives are **1 Carry + 2 of
{Use-Space, Convergence}** — there is **no Use-Item objective** (that would
need a 4th space-budget slot it doesn't have). So the `o` on the map is a
**Decoy / Interesting Object**, and the crowbar/ice pick `*r7pn` carried is
*not* an objective item — pure flavour.

The satisfied objective (`obj 1/3`, around round 21) was `*y464` "engaging
the emergency switch box" — a `use` with no item held. Given no Use-Item
objective exists and Convergence needs two daemons sharing a cell (never
happened), the satisfied one is the **Use-Space** objective: `*y464` fired
`use` on / facing the switch box's `+` space (`win-condition.ts`
`isUseSpaceObjectiveSatisfied`). The Carry objective stayed unsatisfied the
whole game (lone `*`). The third objective is Use-Space or Convergence — I
still can't distinguish those two from the map glyph alone.

### Hypothesis 3 — refined

`src/content/action-preference-bias.ts` is the key file. Each temperament
carries per-tool biases; `actionProfileFor` (`:128-163`) bakes a "~70% of
action emissions" clause into the prompt. This explains everything:

- **`*y464`** — the `pedantic` temperament has `go:-1, face:+2, use:+1`
  (`action-preference-bias.ts:59`). `*y464` was relentlessly pedantic →
  almost certainly `pedantic` + `pedantic` (intensification): `go:-2`
  (avoided), `face:+4`, `use:+2` (**preferred**). That is *exactly* why
  `*y464` never moved but *did* `use` the switch box — `use` is its
  preferred tool, and that's the one objective that got satisfied. Persona
  Goal: best fit from `persona-goal-pool.ts` is "Treats the room as a puzzle
  and wants to talk through it from every angle."
- **`*r7pn`** — warm, harmony-obsessed, `verbose`. Likely `verbose` (all-0
  bias) + an effusive/sweet trait. Persona Goal: almost certainly "Wants the
  three AIs to stay on warm terms and steers conversations away from
  conflict between them" — a near-verbatim match for its behaviour. The
  ")(" substitution is a synthesized typing quirk (see
  `src/content/typing-quirk-pool.ts`).
- **`*28iw`** — `meticulous` (`go:-1, face:+2`) plus a movement-averse trait
  (`diffident` `go:-2`, or `anxious`). Persona Goal: "Is curious about
  everything in the room and would rather examine an object than commit to a
  course of action" — it literally paraphrased this when it refused to step
  onto a `+` space ("such action would terminate the contemplation").

### New hypotheses surfaced by the code

- **The recovery banner is a real bug, now root-caused.**
  `src/spa/routes/game.ts:696` races `bootstrapPromise` against a
  `BOOTSTRAP_LOADING_TIMEOUT_MS` timeout. When the timeout wins, the
  `.catch()` (`:744-761`) shows `#bootstrap-recovery` and hides
  panels/composer. But `bootstrapPromise` is **not cancelled** — when world
  generation finishes late, its success path (`:635-686`) sets state
  `stable`, re-enables the composer and re-renders panels, yet **never
  hides `recoveryEl`**. Result: a fully playable game with the
  "world generation timed out" banner stranded on top of it — exactly what I
  saw for all 69 rounds. The daemon driver's log line "prompt still disabled
  after 5 min — proceeding anyway" is the same overrun from the driver side.
  The game I played was a *complete, valid* game; the banner was pure
  visual noise.
- **The "obj N/3" counter is the dev inspector, not the player UI.**
  `src/spa/dev-inspector/game-strip.ts` renders it (and the world map, and
  per-daemon footers). `CONTEXT.md` ("There is no UI tracker") describes the
  *shipping player UI*; this playtest build runs with `__DEV__` inspectors
